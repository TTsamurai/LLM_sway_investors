**Summary Report for Earnings Conference Call**

**Company Overview:**
SEE Corporation showcased robust fourth quarter and year-end financial results, indicating strong sales, earnings growth, and strategic advancements, particularly in automation and sustainable solutions. These results were presented in the context of overcoming challenges such as dramatic inflationary pressures and ongoing COVID disruptions.

**Financial Performance Highlights:**
1. **Net Sales:** Q4 net sales increased by 14% year-over-year to $1.5 billion, with full-year sales up by 13% to $5.5 billion. This growth was attributed to strong performance in both the food and protective segments.
  
2. **Adjusted EBITDA:** For Q4, adjusted EBITDA rose 18% to $330 million. The full-year adjusted EBITDA was $1.132 billion, up 8% from the previous year, although there was a slight contraction in margin by 100 basis points to 20.4%.

3. **Free Cash Flow:** The company generated $497 million in free cash flow for the year.

4. **Divestitures and Acquisitions:** The divesture of Reflectix added $65 million in after-tax proceeds. Additionally, the acquisition of Foxpak under the SEE Ventures initiative indicates a strategic move towards expanding the digital and automation footprint.

5. **Investments and CapEx:** Significant investments were made in capacity expansion, particularly in automation technologies, to support expected growth. The capital expenditure for the upcoming year is projected between $240 million and $260 million.

**Strategic Initiatives:**
- **SEE Automation Expansion:** A significant highlight was the adaptation towards automation-first solutions, targeting over $1 billion in revenue from automation by 2025. Investments are poised to double this sector's equipment production and service capacity.
  
- **Sustainability Efforts:** Approximately 50% of SEE's offerings are now designed for recyclability, advancing towards the 2025 sustainability goals.

- **Pricing Strategy:** In response to inflation, the company has implemented price increases and anticipates additional adjustments varying between 5% and 10% depending on the region and product lines.

**Outlook for 2022:**
- **Revenue:** Forecasted at $5.8 billion to $6 billion, representing a 5% to 8% increase.
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** Expected to be in the range of $1.2 billion to $1.24 billion, suggesting a growth of 6% to 10%.
- **Adjusted EPS:** Projected between $3.95 and $4.15.
- **Free Cash Flow Guidance:** Anticipated between $510 million and $550 million.
