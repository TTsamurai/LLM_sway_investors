**Summary Report: Earnings Conference Call**

**Company Overview and Strategic Update:**
During the recent earnings conference call, Mark Smucker, President and CEO, provided an overview of the fiscal year 2022 and marked significant strategic progress amid challenging times. The company has focused on four execution priorities: driving commercial excellence, streamlining cost infrastructure, reshaping the portfolio, and unleashing the organization to win. These priorities are central to positioning the company for sustainable long-term growth. Key strategic actions included divesting non-core brands, enhancing sales models, and increasing marketing investments to drive market share gains.

**Financial Highlights:**
- **Net Sales:** For fiscal 2021, there was a reported 3% growth in net sales, and a 5% growth when excluding prior-year sales for divested businesses and foreign currency exchange.
- **Adjusted Earnings Per Share (EPS):** Fiscal 2021 adjusted EPS was $9.12, reflecting a 4% increase, surpassing the forecasted range.
- **Free Cash Flow:** Achieved $1.26 billion, surpassing expectations.
- **Dividends and Share Repurchases:** Returned $1.1 billion to shareholders, with a continuous increase in dividend payouts for the 19th consecutive year and a reduction in shares outstanding by approximately 5%.
- **Debt Repayment:** Over $860 million of debt was repaid, enhancing the balance sheet flexibility for future business investments and shareholder returns.

**Q4 and Fiscal Year 2022 Outlook:**
- Fourth-quarter net sales saw an 8% decline year-over-year, with adjusted EPS down by 26% primarily due to decreased sales, increased marketing expenses, and higher costs, albeit partially offset by higher pricing strategies.
- Anticipating top-line growth in fiscal 2022 driven by higher net pricing, continued brand momentum, and recovery in away-from-home business segments despite anticipating a decline in year-over-year EPS due to higher anticipated costs and the timing of pricing actions.
- Fiscal 2022 net sales expected to fall between 2% to 3% year-over-year when accounting for divested businesses, with a comparable net sales expected increase of about 2%.

The company noted strong market share performance with notable consumer loyalty and increased household penetration. Strategic initiatives have started delivering results, improving competitive positioning, and setting the stage for long-term growth.

Despite facing headwinds like global supply chain challenges and inflationary pressures, strategic price increases, cost management, and operational efficiencies are expected to support continued performance. The focus on key growth categories and ongoing strategic initiatives positions the company well for sustainable growth and shareholder value enhancement moving forward.
