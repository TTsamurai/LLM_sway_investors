### Earnings Conference Call Summary Report: Linde

**Key Financial Metrics:**

1. **Earnings Per Share (EPS):**
   - EPS for the quarter is $2.73, showing a 27% increase over the previous year. This growth in EPS underscores Linde's capability to leverage higher volumes and pricing adjustments against a relatively stable cost base.

2. **Operating Cash Flow:**
   - Linde reported a record operating cash flow of $2.6 billion in the third quarter, attributed to stronger earnings, timing benefits from the previous quarter, and engineering contract prepayments.

3. **Return on Capital (ROC):**
   - The company achieved a notable ROC of 16.7%, representing another record as profit continues to grow double-digit percent over a flat capital base.

4. **Operating Margin:**
   - Operating margin stood at 23.6%, which is 150 basis points higher than in 2020 but 60 basis points lower than the peak in the second quarter. Adjusted for cost pass-through effects, the margin would have shown a 220-basis-point increase over the previous year.

5. **Capital Allocation:**
   - Cash allocations include $2.3 billion invested back into the business and $4.8 billion returned to shareholders through dividends and stock repurchases. The investments displayed on the pie chart exclude sale of plant costs, clarifying that Linde is investing more significantly in contractually secured growth than shown.

6. **Debt Market Activity:**
   - In September, Linde raised nearly EUR two billion through debt issuance across various maturities, with exceptionally low coupons, reflecting strong market confidence in Linde's financial health and future prospects.

7. **Sales and Revenue:**
   - Sales increased by 12% year-over-year to $7.7 billion. This growth is supported by an 8% volume increase across all geographies and markets, coupled with a pricing increase of 3% from the previous year.

8. **Project Backlog:**
   - The project backlog showed significant growth, standing at $13.4 billion, up 81% sequentially from $7.5 billion in the last quarter, indicating secured growth potential.

**Environmental Commitments:**

- Linde has updated its greenhouse gas emission goals, aiming for a 35% reduction in Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions by 2035 (35 by 35 target). Plans include focusing on carbon capture and sequestration, advancing blue and green hydrogen production, transitioning to a zero-emission fleet, and tripling renewable energy sourcing by 2035.

**Leadership Transition:**

- A seamless leadership transition is underway with Sanjiv set to become the CEO effective March 1, following meticulous succession planning. The outgoing CEO will assume the role of Chairman of the Board, maintaining an influential presence.

**Future Outlook:**

- Q4 EPS guidance ranges between $2.60 and $2.70, projecting a 13% to 17% increase over the previous year and a 38% to 43% increase over 2019. Full-year guidance for EPS is adjusted to $10.52 to $10.62, indicating robust year-over-year growth of 28% to 29% and 43% to 45% over 2019.

**Conclusion:**

Despite facing challenging market conditions, Linde continues to demonstrate solid operational performance and financial robustness. The company is well-positioned for sustained growth, supported by a strong project backlog, aggressive capital management, and an enduring commitment to environmental stewardship and shareholder value.
