## Earnings Conference Call Summary Report

### Company Overview
The conference call was led by Mitch Butier, Chairman, President and CEO, and Greg Lovins, SVP and CFO. The discussion included an analysis of the companyâ€™s financial performance, operational challenges, and strategic initiatives.

### Key Financial Highlights
- **Earnings Growth:** Adjusted earnings per share for the quarter rose to $2.14, marking a 12% increase over the previous year, and a 29% increase compared to 2019.
- **Sales Growth:** Sales increased by 17% excluding currency impacts (ex-currency) and by 14% on an organic basis compared to the prior year. Sales growth compared to 2019 was up by 10% organically.
- **Adjusted EBITDA Margin:** The margin for the quarter was recorded at 15.4%, showing a decline of 70 basis points from last year but an increase of 120 basis points compared to 2019.
- **Free Cash Flow:** Year-to-date free cash flow was reported to be $639 million, showing significant growth over previous years.
- **Guidance for Full Year:** Adjusted earnings per share are expected to be between $8.80 and $8.95, with a revised estimate reflecting approximately 15% organic sales growth for the full year.

### Operational Challenges
- **Supply Chain Constraints:** The company faced significant challenges due to raw material shortages, labor constraints, particularly in LGM North America, and operational restrictions due to COVID in RBIS Vietnam.
- **Inflationary Pressures:** Continuing inflationary pressures are observed in the costs of raw materials and freight, notably above expectations, with an annualized inflation impact expected to exceed $600 million.

### Strategic Initiatives and Achievements
- **Price Adjustments:** In response to inflation, the company has implemented price increases and has intensified efforts in material reengineering to mitigate the impact.
- **Acquisitions:** The company closed the acquisition of Vestcom for approximately $1.45 billion, aimed at bolstering the Intelligent Labels strategy.
- **Shareholder Returns:** A total of $290 million was returned to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases.

### Segment Performance
- **Label and Graphic Materials (LGM):** This segment showed strong sales growth, driven by high volume in consumer packaged goods and e-commerce, although profitability was impacted by inflation and supply disruptions.
- **Retail Branding and Information Solutions (RBIS):** The segment reported a 22% growth (constant currency basis), with significant margin expansion, driven by strong performance in the core Apparel business and high-value product categories.
- **Industrial and Healthcare Materials (IHM):** Sales increased by 15% on an organic basis, with growth in both Industrial and Healthcare sectors, albeit with a margin compression due to increased costs.

### Outlook and Projections
- The company adjusted its full-year earnings outlook upwards and anticipates strong sales growth, reflecting robust demand across its business segments. However, challenges such as inflation and supply chain disruptions are expected to persist, impacting operational costs and margins.

### Conclusion
Despite pronounced supply chain and inflationary challenges, the company demonstrated strong financial and operational performance, with effective strategic initiatives in place to mitigate ongoing pressures. The management remains committed to achieving long-term growth and driving shareholder value. The positive revisions in the financial outlook underscore a robust demand environment and operational resilience.
