**Dollar General Earnings Report Summary**

**Performance Overview for Fiscal Year 2021 and Q4:**
- **Net Sales:**
  - FY: Grew 1.4% to $34.2 billion.
  - Q4: Increased by 2.8% to $8.7 billion.
- **Comparable Store Sales:**
  - FY: Decreased by 2.8%.
  - Q4: Decreased by 1.4%.
- **Earnings Per Share (EPS):**
  - FY: Data not specified in detail.
  - Q4: Decreased by 1.9% to $2.57, but increased by a CAGR of 10.6% over two years.
- **Operating Profit:**
  - Q4: Decreased by 8.7% to $797 million.
- **Gross Profit Margin:**
  - Q4: Fell 131 basis points, partly due to increased supply chain expenses ($100 million headwind).

**Strategic Highlights and Adjustments:**
- Successful control of inflationary pressures through strong pricing strategies.
- Notable market share gains in frozen and refrigerated categories, despite a marginal decrease in highly consumable product sales.
- Successful retention of new customers acquired in 2020, with higher loyalty than expected.
- Expanded pOpshelf concept, exceeding the opening target with 55 locations now operational.
- Implementation of DG Fresh across the chain, leading to cost savings and increased sales in frozen and refrigerated goods.
- Over 2,900 real estate projects completed, including 1,050 new stores.

**Financial Strategy and Shareholder Value:**
- Continued investment in high-return growth opportunities.
- Share repurchases: Repurchased 2.2 million shares in Q4 for $490 million.
- Dividends: Paid quarterly dividend of $0.42 per share, totaling $97 million in Q4.
- Increased future dividend forecast to $0.55 per share.

**Fiscal Year 2022 Outlook:**
- Anticipated net sales growth of approximately 10% with a benefit from an additional week in the fiscal calendar.
- Forecast same-store sales growth around 2.5%.
- Expected EPS growth of 12% to 14%, influenced positively by the additional fiscal week.
- Capital expenditure predicted between $1.4 billion and $1.5 billion.
- Emphasis on continued growth through the pOpshelf expansion and international ventures, with plans to open stores in Mexico.

**Challenges and Mitigations:**
- Ongoing impacts from inflation, supply chain disruptions, and economic uncertainties due to global events and COVID-19.
- Focused on managing rising costs through strategic initiatives like DG Fresh and expanding the private fleet to control costs and increase distribution efficiency.

**Conclusion:**
Dollar General concludes the fiscal year on a strong note despite various external pressures, with a strategic focus on growth, cost management, and shareholder returns poised to continue through 2022. The company remains committed to its mission and anticipates strengthened market positioning through meticulous execution of its outlined strategic and operational priorities.
