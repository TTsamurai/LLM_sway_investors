### Summary Report: Textron's Quarterly Earnings Conference Call

#### Key Financial Metrics:
1. **Revenue Growth:**
   - Overall revenue increased to $3 billion this quarter, up from $2.7 billion in the same quarter the previous year, marking an increase of approximately 11.1%.
   - Specific segment revenues:
     - Textron Aviation: $1.2 billion, up $386 million year-over-year.
     - Bell: $769 million, down $24 million year-over-year.
     - Textron Systems: $299 million, down $3 million year-over-year.
     - Industrial: $730 million, down $102 million year-over-year.

2. **Earnings Per Share (EPS):**
   - Reported income from continuing operations at $0.82 per share.
   - Adjusted income from continuing operations at $0.85 per share, up from $0.53 per share in the same quarter last year.

3. **Segment Profit:**
   - Total segment profit rose to $279 million, up $90 million from the previous year.
   - Textron Aviation segment profit: $98 million, up $127 million year-over-year.
   - Bell segment profit: $105 million, down $14 million year-over-year.
   - Textron Systems segment profit: $45 million, up $5 million year-over-year.
   - Industrial segment profit: $23 million, down $35 million year-over-year.

4. **Backlogs:**
   - Aviation ended the quarter with a backlog of $3.5 billion.
   - Bell's backlog stood at $4.1 billion.
   - Textron Systems' backlog was $2.2 billion.

5. **Cash Flow:**
   - Manufacturing cash flow before pension contributions was $271 million during the quarter and totaled $851 million year-to-date.
   - Significant improvement compared to the $129 million for the corresponding nine-month period in 2020.

6. **Shareholder returns:**
   - The company repurchased approximately 4.2 million shares, returning $299 million in cash to shareholders this quarter.

7. **Guidance Revision:**
   - Raised the full-year adjusted EPS guidance to a range of $3.20 to $3.30 per share.
   - Outlook for manufacturing cash flow before pension contributions is now in the range of $1 billion to $1.1 billion, up $200 million from the previous outlook.

#### Operational Highlights:
- **Aviation Segment:** Continued strong recovery observed in the general aviation market. Significant increase in Citation jet deliveries and robust backlog growth.
- **Bell Segment:** Faced a slight decrease, largely due to reduced military revenues, though this was partially offset by new initiatives in FARA and ongoing improvements.
- **Textron Systems:** Somewhat stable despite minor revenue falls, owing to the mixture of increased ATAC services and a drop from U.S. military engagement reduction impacts.
- **Industrial:** Challenged by supply chain disruptions affecting production, though countered by improved pricing strategies in Specialized Vehicles.

#### Conclusion:
Textron demonstrated a solid performance in the third quarter, highlighted by increased backlog in aviation, enhanced manufacturing margins, and sustained strong cash generation. The company continues to navigate operational challenges, particularly in supply chain management, with strategic fiscal approaches, anticipating continued growth and shareholder returns in the forthcoming periods.
