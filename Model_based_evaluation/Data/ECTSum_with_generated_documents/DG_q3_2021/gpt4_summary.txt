### Key Financial Metrics:
- **Net Sales**: Increased by 3.9% to $8.5 billion, following a 17.3% increase in the prior year period.
- **Comp Sales**: Declined by 0.6% compared to the prior year but represented a robust 11.6% increase on a two-year stack basis.
- **Gross Profit Margin**: 30.8%, down 57 basis points from FY2020, mainly due to higher LIFO provision, increased transportation costs, and greater proportion of consumables sales.
- **Operating Profit**: Decreased 13.9% to $665.6 million, with operating margin dropping to 7.8%.
- **Earnings Per Share (EPS)**: $2.08, down 10%, growth rate of approximately 21% over a two-year period.
- **Merchandise Inventories**: $5.3 billion, up 5.4% overall, with a slight decrease on a per-store basis.

### Updates and Strategic Initiatives:
1. **Real Estate Expansion**: Planning nearly 3,000 real estate projects in FY2022 including 1,100 new store openings. The expansion includes the acceleration of the pOpshelf concept, targeting nearly 1,000 locations by FY2025.
2. **International Expansion**: Early stages of plans to extend footprint into Mexico, targeting the opening of pilot stores in 2022.
3. **Digital Transformation**: Introduced digital services with a partnership with DoorDash for delivery in under an hour at over 10,000 locations.
4. **Product Assortment**: Approximately 20% of the overall product assortment remains at $1 or less, affirming commitment to affordable pricing.
5. **Strategic Initiatives**:
   - **DG Fresh**: Completed rollout across the chain, continuing to see significant cost savings and margin benefits.
   - **pOpshelf Concept**: Exceeding expectations in sales and gross margin, planning for significant expansion.
   - **NCI (Non-Consumables Initiative)**: Contributed approximately 2.5% comp sales increase in involved stores, with plans for further expansion.
   - **Health and Wellness Expansion**: Expanding health offerings, especially in rural America.

### Financial Outlook for FY2021:
- **Net Sales Growth**: Expected between 1% to 1.5%.
- **Same-Store Sales**: Anticipated decline of 2.5% to 3%, but reflects a growth of 13% to 14% on a two-year basis.
- **EPS Guidance**: Now estimated in the range of $9.90 to $10.20, reflecting an effective tax rate of approximately 22%.

### Challenges:
- Ongoing pressures from cost inflation and supply chain disruptions impacting gross margins, offset by higher inventory markups and reduced shrink.

### Conclusion:
Dollar General demonstrated resilience with a continued focus on strategic initiatives to foster growth, despite facing headwinds like supply chain disruptions and cost inflation. The expansion plans, both domestically and now internationally with a potential move into Mexico, alongside robust digital integration strategies, highlight the company's proactive approach to growth and market leadership in the discount retail sector.
