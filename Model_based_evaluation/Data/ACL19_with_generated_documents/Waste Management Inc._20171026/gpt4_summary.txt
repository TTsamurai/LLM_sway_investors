**Summary Report on Earnings Conference Call for Waste Management for Q3**

**Financial Highlights:**

1. **Operating EBITDA and Revenue:**
   - Operating EBITDA increased by approximately 7% to $1.071 billion compared to Q3 of the previous year, marking the second consecutive quarter of record operating EBITDA.
   - Revenue grew by $168 million or 4.7%, primarily driven by yield and volume growth in the traditional solid waste business. The recycling business contributed $52 million to revenue growth.

2. **Free Cash Flow:**
   - Free cash flow conversion rate was close to 50%, with an 18% growth in free cash flow compared to the same period last year. The company raised its full-year free cash flow guidance to $1.7 billion to $1.75 billion, up from $1.5 billion to $1.6 billion.

3. **Earnings Per Share (EPS):**
   - EPS for the quarter was $0.90, a 7.1% increase compared to Q3 of 2016. This increase occurred despite a negative $0.01 impact from hurricanes and the expiration of fuel tax credits. Full-year EPS guidance was raised to $3.19 to $3.21, up from $3.14 to $3.18.

4. **Pricing and Margin Enhancements:**
   - The core price increase was 4.7%, and the yield was 2%. The operating income grew by 8.3%, and the operating margin improved by 60 basis points.

5. **Recycling Business:**
   - Despite commodity price pressures, recycling earnings grew slightly over $0.01 per share from the same quarter last year. However, recycled MRF tons declined by 9.1% due to hurricane impacts.
   - Challenging market conditions expected due to China’s ban on certain recyclable materials. The company expects a $0.04 decline in recycling earnings in 2018, heavily weighted to the first half of the year.

**Operational Highlights:**

1. **Strategic Appointments:**
   - Nikolaj Sjoqvist was promoted to Chief Digital Officer to lead the company’s focus on leveraging digital technologies for business growth and operational efficiency.

2. **Impact of Natural Disasters:**
   - Hurricanes Harvey and Irma, as well as other natural disasters, negatively impacted the business but also presented opportunities for increased debris management and recycling activities.

3. **Capital Expenditure and Investments:**
   - CapEx is expected to be at the high end of the $1.4 billion to $1.5 billion range guided at the start of the year. Investments are being directed towards enhancing the CNG fleet, which shows compelling returns compared to traditional diesel trucks.

4. **Strategic Growth and Market Positioning:**
   - The company continues to focus on reducing operational costs, enhancing customer service through technological advancements, and maintaining robust growth from core operations without relying heavily on acquisitions.

**Forward-Looking Statements:**
- Despite some uncertainties, the company remains optimistic about continuing solid performance based on volume growth and effective pricing strategies. More specific guidance for 2018 will be provided in the forthcoming quarterly earnings call.

**Conclusion:**
Waste Management has demonstrated a strong operational and financial performance in the third quarter, despite significant challenges from natural disasters. The company is well-positioned for continued growth with strategic focus areas on digital transformation, pricing discipline, and operational efficiencies.
