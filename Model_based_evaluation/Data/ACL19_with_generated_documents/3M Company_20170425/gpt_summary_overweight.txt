The company had a strong first quarter with organic growth of 4.6%, driven by strong performance across all geographic areas. The U.S. saw organic growth of 1.4%, with mid-single-digit increases in Industrial and Safety & Graphics, solid growth in Electronics & Energy and Health Care, and a decline in the Consumer business due to channel adjustments in the office market.

Asia-Pacific led the company with organic growth of 10.1%, with double-digit increases in Electronics & Energy, and strong growth in Industrial, Health Care, and Consumer. Europe, Middle East, and Africa saw organic growth increase 4%, driven by Industrial and Safety & Graphics. Latin America/Canada had organic growth of 2.3%, with strong growth in Health Care, Consumer, and Safety & Graphics.

First quarter sales were $7.7 billion, and net income increased 3.7% to $1.3 billion. GAAP operating margins were 23.1%, down 100 basis points versus last year's Q1. Excluding the impact of strategic investments, margins were up 80 basis points year over year, driven by strong organic growth and solid operational performance.

Earnings per share increased 5.4% to $2.16, with organic growth and productivity contributing $0.22 per share to Q1 earnings. The full-year tax rate is expected to be in the range of 26% to 27.5%.

Free cash flow was $701 million, with full-year expectations for free cash flow conversion to be in the range of 95% to 105%. First quarter capital expenditures were $287 million, and full-year investments are expected to be in the range of $1.3 billion to $1.5 billion.

The Industrial business group saw strong organic growth of 5.7%, with the automotive OEM business leading sales growth. Safety & Graphics had first quarter sales of $1.5 billion, up 4.8% organically, with personal safety and roofing granules driving growth. Health Care generated first quarter sales of $1.4 billion, with organic growth of 3.1% year on year. Electronics & Energy led the company with organic growth of 11.5%, and Consumer had first quarter sales of $1 billion, with organic growth declining 1.2% year on year.

The company expects organic growth for Industrial and Safety & Graphics to be between 2% and 5%, Health Care in the range of 3% to 5%, Electronics & Energy between 1% to 6%, and Consumer in the range of 1% to 3%.

The company is making strategic investments to accelerate growth and optimize its portfolio and supply chain footprint. These investments are expected to add between $0.05 and $0.10 to 2017 earnings per share, with incremental costs in the range of $0.40 to $0.45 per share over the remainder of the year.

Overall, the company's strong performance in the first quarter, coupled with its strategic investments, positions it well for continued growth and value enhancement. As a financial analyst, I recommend an 'overweight' investment stance based on the solid financial metrics and growth prospects.
