Summary:
- 3M posted strong organic growth in the first quarter of 4.6%, with volumes up 4.5% and selling prices up 0.1%.
- Total first quarter sales in U.S. dollars increased 3.7% versus last year, with growth across all geographic areas.
- First quarter GAAP operating margins were 23.1%, down 100 basis points versus last year's Q1, impacted by strategic investments and higher pension and OPEB expense.
- First quarter GAAP earnings increased 5.4%, to $2.16 per share, driven by organic growth and productivity.
- The first quarter tax rate was 23.7%, versus 26.8% in the comparable quarter, expected to be in the range of 26% to 27.5% for 2017.
- First quarter free cash flow was $701 million, down $245 million year on year, largely due to the timing of pension contributions.
- Full-year 2017 pension contributions are expected to remain in the range of $300 million to $500 million, similar to 2016.
- First quarter capital expenditures were $287 million, in line with expectations, with full-year investments anticipated in the range of $1.3 billion to $1.5 billion.
- First quarter per share dividend was increased by 6%, resulting in $702 million in cash dividends paid to shareholders during the quarter.
- $690 million was returned to shareholders through gross share repurchases during the quarter.
- Industrial continued its strong growth momentum, delivering first quarter sales of $2.7 billion, up 5.7% organically, with operating margins of 23.1%.
- Safety & Graphics saw first quarter sales up 4.8% organically to $1.5 billion, with operating margins increasing 180 basis points to 26.1%.
- Health Care generated first quarter sales of $1.4 billion, with organic growth of 3.1% and operating margins of 30.5%.
- Electronics & Energy led the company with organic growth of 11.5% in the first quarter, with sales of $1.2 billion and operating margins of 18.6%.
- Consumer saw first quarter sales of $1 billion, with organic growth declining 1.2% year on year, and operating margins of 21.3%.
- Strategic investments impacted margins by 180 basis points, and higher pension and OPEB expense decreased margins by 30 basis points year on year.
- Organic growth and productivity contributed 120 basis points to margins, while lower raw material costs added another 50 basis points.
- Foreign currency, net of hedge gains, brought margins down 40 basis points in the quarter.

Overall, 3M delivered strong performance in the first quarter, with growth in all business groups and geographic areas. The company's strategic investments and cost management initiatives are expected to drive future growth and productivity. However, the impact of foreign currency and pension contributions may continue to influence margins and earnings per share for the remainder of the year.
