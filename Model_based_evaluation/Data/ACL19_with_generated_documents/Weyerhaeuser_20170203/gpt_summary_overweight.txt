Summary Report:

Weyerhaeuser had a transformational year in 2016, with a focus on timberland and forest products after merging with Plum Creek and divesting the Cellulose Fibers business. The company reported a strong performance with increased full-year EBITDA by almost 55% to nearly $1.6 billion. Net earnings for the full-year 2016 were just over $1 billion or $1.39 per diluted share on net sales of $6.4 billion. 

The fourth quarter net earnings were $551 million or $0.73 per diluted share on net sales of $1.6 billion. Adjusted EBITDA for the fourth quarter totaled $400 million, an improvement of over 60% compared with a year ago. There was a focus on key financial metrics, including strong performance in timberlands, wood products, real estate, energy, and natural resources. 

The company reported significant operational excellence improvements and cost synergies, exceeding their initial expectations. Single family housing activity continued to see steady improvement in the fourth quarter, and the company is optimistic about the opportunities in 2017, driven by strong housing markets and improving timberland markets. The company remains confident in its ability to achieve its operational excellence targets and is focused on disciplined capital allocation to drive superior value for shareholders. 

The key financial metrics for the company's performance in 2016 and its outlook for 2017 support an overweight investment stance. The company's strong operational excellence, cost synergies, and growth opportunities make it an attractive investment option. The company's ability to capitalize on improving markets, drive value for shareholders, and deliver industry-leading performance further supports an overweight investment stance.
