Based on the recent earnings call, it is recommended to consider an 'overweight' investment stance on the company. The Q2 results show strong performance, with revenue of $1.5 billion, up 5% versus last year, and GAAP operating earnings of $257 million, up 15% from last year. Additionally, GAAP earnings per share were $0.78, up 28% from $0.61 in the year-ago quarter.

The company's Products segment saw significant growth in every region, reflecting strong demand for P25 LMR systems. Product backlog ended the quarter at $1.6 billion, up 15% from last year, driven by North America. Additionally, the company secured notable product wins, including a $43 million award for P25 devices in a major U.S. city and a $40 million P25 system in Broward County, Florida.

In Services, the company experienced growth in Managed & Support Services, with a 3% increase in revenue or 7% in constant currency. Services backlog ended at $6.9 billion, up $61 million from last year.

Looking at the company's outlook, it is expected that Q3 sales will be up 3% to 4% versus last year, and non-GAAP EPS between $1.36 and $1.41. For the full year, the company is raising both revenue and EPS guidance, now expecting full-year 2017 sales growth of 3% to 4% and non-GAAP EPS of $5.20 to $5.30.

In conclusion, with strong Q2 results and a positive outlook for the remainder of the year, it is recommended to consider an 'overweight' investment stance on the company. The company's performance in both Products and Services, along with its successful product wins, indicate potential for continued growth and success in the future.
