Based on the earnings conference call transcript, PPL had a strong quarter with solid results. They announced reported earnings of $0.43 per share for the second quarter of 2017, compared to $0.71 per share in the previous year. Year-to-date reported earnings were $1.01 per share, compared to $1.41 per share in the first half of 2016. Adjusting for special items, second quarter 2017 earnings from ongoing operations were $0.52 per share, compared with $0.56 per share a year ago. The full 2017 ongoing earnings forecast is $2.05 to $2.25 per share, with a midpoint of $2.15 per share. PPL expects 5% to 6% compound annual earnings per share growth from 2017 through 2020, with annual dividend growth of about 4% a year through 2020.

In Kentucky, the Kentucky Public Service Commission authorized a combined rate increase of $116 million for Kentucky Utilities and Louisville Gas and Electric. This is expected to support continued investments in safe, reliable electricity and natural gas service. LG&E recently completed a 540-mile, 20-year gas-main replacement project, enhancing the safety and reliability of gas service in Louisville. In Pennsylvania, PPL Electric Utilities ranked highest among large utilities in the East and continues to enhance its Smart Grid capabilities.

In the U.K., Ofgem issued an open letter seeking feedback from stakeholders to construct a framework for RIIO-2, which will begin in the first quarter of 2018. PPL remains confident in its ability to navigate through new price controls and work with Ofgem to achieve a fair outcome. 

Overall, PPL is executing well on its low-risk business plans, delivering for customers, and targeting earnings and dividend growth, which suggests a positive outlook for the company's future performance. Therefore, adopting an 'overweight' investment stance on PPL would be appropriate, given the company's strong financial performance and growth prospects.
