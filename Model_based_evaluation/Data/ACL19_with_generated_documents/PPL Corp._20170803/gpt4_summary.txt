**PPL Corporation Earnings Summary Report Q2 2017**

**Overview:**
During the recent earnings conference call, PPL Corporation provided a detailed update on its financial performance for the second quarter and the first half of 2017, alongside updates to its full-year earnings guidance and operational highlights both in the U.S. and the U.K.

**Key Financial Metrics:**
- **Reported Earnings (Q2 2017):** $0.43 per share, a decrease from $0.71 per share in Q2 2016.
- **Reported Earnings (Year-to-date 2017):** $1.01 per share, down from $1.41 per share in the first half of 2016.
- **Earnings from Ongoing Operations (Q2 2017):** Adjusted to $0.52 per share compared to $0.56 per share in Q2 2016.
- **Earnings from Ongoing Operations (Year-to-date 2017):** $1.14 per share, down from $1.23 per share in the same period last year.

**Factors Influencing Earnings:**
- A significant factor in the reduced earnings both quarterly and year-to-date was the lower foreign currency exchange rates, which negatively impacted earnings from ongoing operations by $0.07 per share in Q2 2017, and by $0.17 per share year-to-date.
- Partially offsetting these impacts were lower operating and maintenance expenses and the effects of the annual price increase in the U.K. effective from April 1, 2016.
- Unfavorable weather in Kentucky also contributed to the lower earnings by reducing electricity sales volumes.

**2017 Earnings Guidance:**
PPL reaffirmed its ongoing earnings forecast for 2017, projecting $2.05 to $2.25 per share, with a midpoint of $2.15 per share. Specific adjustments included:
- **Kentucky segment:** Revised ongoing earnings projection to $0.56 per share, primarily due to lower electricity sales resulting from unfavorable weather.
- **U.K. Regulated segment:** Expected stronger performance with a projection of $1.20 per share, benefiting from lower operating expenses and reduced income taxes.

**Operational Highlights:**
- In Kentucky, regulatory approvals resulted in a rate increase and set the return on equity at 9.7%.
- Significant infrastructure investments were highlighted, including a completed 540-mile gas-main replacement project by LG&E.
- Recognition for customer service excellence in both the Kentucky and Pennsylvania operations, with multiple J.D. Power awards.

**Growth and Investment Projections:**
Looking forward, PPL continues to target a 5% to 6% compound annual earnings growth per share from 2017 through 2020, supported by over $13 billion in planned infrastructure investments. Dividend growth is targeted at approximately 4% annually through 2020.

**U.K. Regulatory Developments:**
- Ofgem's RIIO-2 process is in the early stages, seeking stakeholder feedback to shape the framework that will take effect in April 2023.
- PPL plans active engagement in the consultation process and anticipates that its experience with current price controls will aid in effectively navigating the new framework.

**Conclusion:**
Despite the challenges posed by external factors like unfavorable weather and currency fluctuations, PPL Corporation remains on track to meet its financial targets for 2017 and continue its growth trajectory through 2020. The company's strategy includes significant infrastructure development, regulatory engagement, and maintaining high standards of customer service, which collectively should support ongoing financial health and shareholder value.
