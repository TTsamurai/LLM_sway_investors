Adjusted gross margin was 49% which is 70 basis points higher than last year, driven by acquisitions and improved manufacturing costs. Adjusted operating margin was 22%, about 20 basis points lower than last year, mainly due to higher R&D spend relative to the prior year. Adjusted net income for the third quarter of 2017 was up 11% to $72.7 million and adjusted EPS was $0.52 per share, 8% higher than last year. Quarterly cash flow from operations was 143% of adjusted net income or $104 million, which was $15 million higher than the prior year. Operating cash flow was 116% of adjusted net income through nine months of 2017. Despite repaying outstanding borrowings under the revolving credit facility and returning $21 million to shareholders in the form of dividends, the company increased its cash balance by $33 million to $437 million at the end of the quarter. Geographically, revenue increased 9% in the U.S. and 27% in Europe, with weakness in the Middle East region due to slower activity in the Surveillance segment. The company has achieved an impressive book-to-bill ratio of 1.45 times and increased backlog by 21% quarter-over-quarter to $395 million. Orders from the U.S. Army and the Australian Army have contributed to the growth in the Surveillance segment. The Detection segment saw revenue grow by 19% year-over-year to $31.4 million, with a deteriorated backlog due to the shipments of the DR-SKO under the previous order. Instruments revenue of $91.4 million was up 11% over the third quarter of 2016, with particular strength in both volume and premium handheld plant predictive maintenance cameras as well as growth in automation cameras. The OEM and emerging segment saw revenue increase by 39% over last year, with a slight organic revenue increase. Security segment revenue of $65.7 million was up 16% compared to last year's third quarter, with significant growth in the LOREX web channel and FLIR-branded Professional Visible Security solutions. Maritime segment revenue was $42.3 million, up 4% versus the prior year, with strong gross margins mix and controlled operations spending resulting in operating margin improvement by 280 basis points compared to Q3 of 2016. The company also has significant firepower for acquisitions, being investment grade rated and intending to continue being acquisitive. Overall, the company's financial metrics demonstrate strong growth and profitability, making it an attractive investment opportunity.
