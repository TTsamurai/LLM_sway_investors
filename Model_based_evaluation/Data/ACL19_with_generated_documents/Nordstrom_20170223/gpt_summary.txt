Summary Report:
- Nordstrom's 2016 results demonstrated the company's agility in responding to changes in business conditions and making fundamental improvements to its operating model.
- The company made aggressive actions to prioritize resources and optimize inventory turns, resulting in stabilized retail operating margins over the past three quarters.
- Nordstrom generated operating cash flow of $1.6 billion for the year, well surpassing historical averages, largely driven by a 2.5% reduction in inventory and a 20% reduction in CapEx.
- The fourth quarter earnings exceeded expectations, reflecting continued momentum in inventory and expense execution, with a gross profit expansion of over 100 basis points.
- Nordstrom achieved record sales of $14.5 billion for the year, and made investments to fuel growth areas, including expansion into Canada, online business growth, and off-price business growth.
- The company's business model has shifted from a four-wall model to one that supports multiple channels, with e-commerce now representing nearly one-quarter of the business.
- The company's financial outlook for 2017 includes earnings per diluted share of $2.75 to $3.00, with total sales growth of 3% to 4% and flat comp sales.
- Nordstrom expects technology and supply chain investments to support multi-channel growth, with a capital plan of roughly $3.4 billion over the next five years, allocating approximately 40% towards modernizing the tech platform and digital enhancements.
- The company is making progress in improving the efficiency of its e-commerce business and expects improved operating performance from its investments in new market opportunities.
- Nordstrom has seen good growth in its online off-price business and is making progress in scaling its Nordstromrack.com and HauteLook businesses.
- The loyalty program, launched in 2016, has been successful in extending the company's relationship with its customers, with loyalty customers spending three to four times more than non-loyalty customers.
- The company plans to integrate the two loyalty models on one platform to further improve the customer experience.
- Nordstrom's Neiman event and the role of Geevy's has not had a measurable impact on the company's performance.
