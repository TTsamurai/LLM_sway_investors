- The company has shown continued momentum in the third quarter, with all segments contributing to a growth rate of 2.1% versus the prior year.
- The triple-net business, which represents 38% of the company's NOI, grew same-store cash NOI by an attractive 3.8% in the third quarter of 2017, driven primarily by in-place lease escalations.
- Cash flow coverage in the overall stabilized triple-net lease portfolio was 1.6 times sequentially.
- The triple-net seniors housing portfolio showed steady same-store EBITDA coverage at around 1.3 times.
- The company is excluding assets from its triple-net coverage and same-store supplemental reporting as it enters into a joint venture for senior housing assets currently leased to Elmcroft.
- In the post-acute business, IRF and LTAC coverage declined in the quarter due to rent increases, the continued impact of reimbursement changes, and labor wage pressures. However, the company expects to realize benefits from patient criteria mitigation in coverage ratios starting in the first half of 2018.
- Revenue and EBITDA improvements in the Ardent segment ranked among the top performers in the industry.
- The company is raising its full-year 2017 triple-net same store NOI guidance by 25 basis points at the midpoint to now grow between 3% and 3.5%.
- In the senior housing operating portfolio, the company saw a 0.6% growth in same-store cash NOI during the third quarter.
- Occupancy increased sequentially by 40 basis points to 88.7% in the third quarter, and rate growth was solid at nearly 4%.
- The company has expanded its disclosures on tenant diversification and credit to highlight the quality of its office portfolio.
- Total direct costs resulting from hurricanes Harvey and Irma, including property damage and other expenses, approximated $10 million in the third quarter of 2017.
- Income from continuing operations per share grew 5% in the third quarter, while normalized FFO per share grew 1%.
- The company funded investments of over $80 million during and immediately after the quarter, including life science development spend for projects currently underway.
- It has received $570 million of the $700 million of value from the Kindred SNF sales at a 7% cash and 8% GAAP yield, thereby reducing its SNF NOI exposure to nearly 1%.
- The company is anticipating total portfolio same-store cash NOI to grow between 2% and 2.5% and forecasts normalized FFO per share to range from $4.13 to $4.16.
- The company's financial position at quarter end includes improvement in net debt-to-EBITDA to 5.7 times, lower total indebtedness to gross asset value of 39%, and steady fixed charge coverage at 4.6 times.
- The company is pleased with its performance in 2017 and remains focused on maintaining its excellence.
- The company has entered into a joint venture for over 70 senior housing assets currently leased to Elmcroft, which highlights the value of its portfolio.
