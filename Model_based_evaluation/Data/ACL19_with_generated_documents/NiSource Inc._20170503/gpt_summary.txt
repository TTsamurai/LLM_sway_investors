Based on the earnings conference call transcripts, the key financial metrics for NiSource include:

1. Non-GAAP net operating earnings: In the first quarter of 2017, NiSource reported non-GAAP net operating earnings of approximately $231 million, or $0.71 per share, compared to about $198 million, or $0.62 per share, for the same period in 2016.

2. Operating income: On an operating earnings basis, NiSource reported about $447 million for the quarter, an increase of about $48 million over the same period in 2016. On a GAAP basis, the operating income was about $417 million for the quarter, compared to about $381 million in the first quarter of 2016.

3. Gas Distribution Operations segment: This segment delivered operating earnings of about $364 million in the quarter, an increase of about $34 million compared with the same period in 2016. Excluding the impact of trackers, net revenues were up about $63 million, driven primarily by new rates from base rate cases and infrastructure replacement program.

4. Electric Operations segment: Reported operating earnings of about $84 million in the quarter, an increase of about $12 million from the first quarter of 2016. Excluding the impact of trackers, net revenues were up about $24 million, driven by new rates from the base rate case and increased capital spending on transmission projects.

Debt and credit profile: NiSource's debt level as of March 31 was about $7.9 billion, with a weighted average maturity on long-term debt of approximately 13 years and a weighted average interest rate of approximately 5.4%. 6. Financial outlook: NiSource's credit ratings at the three major agencies are investment grade, with stable outlooks. The company filed an ATM equity issuance program with the SEC as part of its balanced, predictable approach to financing infrastructure investment programs. The long-term earnings and dividend annual growth rates are projected to be 5% to 7% through 2020.
