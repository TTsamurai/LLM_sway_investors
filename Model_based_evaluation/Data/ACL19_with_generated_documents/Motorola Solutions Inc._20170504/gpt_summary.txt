Based on the Q1 results from the earnings conference call transcripts, the key financial metrics are as follows:

- Q1 revenue of $1.3 billion, up 7% from last year
- GAAP operating earnings of $176 million, up 76% from last year
- Non-GAAP operating earnings of $224 million, or 17.5% of sales, an increase of 360 basis points from the year-ago quarter
- GAAP earnings per share from continuing operations were $0.45 compared to $0.10 in the first quarter of 2016
- Non-GAAP EPS was $0.71 versus $0.52 in the year-ago quarter, a 37% year-over-year increase
- Ending backlog is up $129 million from last year, with product backlog up $285 million and services backlog down $156 million due to a reduction in Airwave backlog
- Q1 product sales were $703 million, up $1 million from last year
- Q1 product segment operating income was $101 million or 14.4% of sales, up 240 basis points from last year
- Q1 services revenue was $578 million, up 18%
- Services operating income was $123 million, up $41 million from the prior year
- Operating expenses (OpEx) were $352 million, a decrease of $3 million from the year-ago quarter
- Q1 operating cash flow was $142 million, an increase of $129 million, driven by higher earnings and a $52 million legal settlement
- Free cash flow was $74 million, an increase of $112 million from last year
- Cash balance at the end of Q1 was $829 million with net debt of $3.6 billion
- During the quarter, the company repurchased $178 million of stock, paid $77 million of dividends, and invested $55 million of cash in acquisitions

In terms of outlook, the company expects Q2 sales to be up 2% to 3% and non-GAAP EPS between $0.98 and $1.03. The full year 2017 sales are expected to be at the high end of the prior range, approximately 2%, with non-GAAP EPS in the range of $5.08 to $5.23.

Key regional results include 3% revenue growth in the Americas, 27% revenue growth in EMEA, and flat revenue in Asia Pac. The company also highlighted significant segment highlights including new product launches and acquisitions in both the product and services segments.

Overall, the company continues to expect a strong performance in operating cash flow for the full year and anticipates positive results in the managed and support services segment.
