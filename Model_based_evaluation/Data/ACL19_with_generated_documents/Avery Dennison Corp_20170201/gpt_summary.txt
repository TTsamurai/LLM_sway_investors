Based on the recent earnings conference call transcripts, the key financial metrics indicate a positive performance for the company. The adjusted earnings per share increased by 17% in Q4 compared to the prior year, driven by higher than expected organic sales growth of approximately 5%. Acquisitions also contributed to sales growth, while currency represented a slight headwind. The adjusted operating margin improved by 70 basis points to 9.4% in the fourth quarter, driven by higher volume and productivity initiatives.

The company's free cash flow was $139 million in the quarter, and $387 million for the full year, driven by higher income and working capital productivity. The company is also committed to the disciplined return of cash to shareholders, repurchasing 3.8 million shares in 2016 at a cost net of proceeds from stock options of $191 million, and paying $143 million in dividends.

Segment performance highlights include a 7% organic sales growth in Label and Graphic Materials, driven by improved volumes and matured markets. The Industrial and Healthcare Materials segment saw a decline in sales but is expected to return to growth in the second half of 2017. The Retail Branding and Information Solutions segment experienced a 5% organic sales growth, with strong growth in RFID. The company also outlined its outlook for 2017, with estimated adjusted earnings per share in the range of $4.30 to $4.50, and an estimated 3% to 4.5% organic sales growth.

Overall, the company's results represent progress towards its long-term targets, with a strong balance sheet and ample capacity for investments. The company anticipates continued profitable growth and is confident in its ability to deliver exceptional value over the long run.
