Summary:
- The company reported a net loss of $1.04 billion or $4.06 per share in the third quarter, compared to a net income of $70.6 million or $0.25 per share in the prior year.
- Operating net loss was $1.03 billion or $4 per share, compared to operating income of $122.5 million or $0.44 per share in the prior year.
- Results were largely driven by the pretax net loss of $1.48 billion associated with natural catastrophes.
- Year-to-date annualized operating ROE, excluding AOCI and integration costs, was negative 8% compared to positive 6.1% in the same period last year.
- Underwriting profitability improved in the quarter, with the combined ratio decreasing from 91.3% to 89.8%.
- Total pretax net catastrophe losses in the third quarter were $1.48 billion, representing 58.8 loss ratio points in the current quarter compared to 4.1 loss ratio points in the third quarter of 2016.
- The majority of losses were related to hurricanes Harvey, Irma, and Maria.
- Operating expenses were $409.4 million in the quarter, a 19.5% or $99.1 million favorable comparison to the prior year quarter.
- Net investment income increased by $1 million to $172 million compared to the prior year quarter.
- The company repurchased approximately 2.7 million common shares early in the third quarter and approximately 13.8 million common shares year-to-date ending September 30.
- The company maintains surplus capital relative to its binding constraints at any time, including those imposed by regulators, rating agencies, and internal capital model.

Overall, the company reported a significant net loss in the third quarter due to natural catastrophes, despite improvements in underwriting profitability and favorable development in the investment portfolio. The company also continues to maintain surplus capital and has repurchased a significant number of its common shares, while expecting to renew its Form S-3 shelf registration in the near future.
