Key financial metrics from the recent earnings conference call transcripts include:

- Enrollment growth in the Government Business, with a total 2016 enrollment increase of 614,000 members, representing 6.9% versus year-end 2015. This resulted in operating revenue of $45.5 billion, a growth of 11.4% versus 2015.

- Government Business operating margins of 3.9% in 2016, a decline of 90 basis points compared to the prior year, driven by lower gross margins in the Medicaid business.

- Medicare Business margins reflected improvement versus 2015, a direct result of investments made in the Medicare business over the past three years.

- Commercial enrollment growth of over 700,000 members during 2016, representing a 2.4% increase, leading to operating revenue of $38.7 billion, an increase of 3% compared to 2015.

- Commercial operating margin improvement to 8.3% in 2016 compared to 7.6% in 2015, driven by lower administrative costs and fixed cost leveraging on a growing membership base.

- The favorable prior-year reserve development of $850 million in 2016, slightly better than expectations.

- Operating cash flow of approximately $3.2 billion for the full year 2016, reflecting the quality of earnings.

- Debt to cap ratio of 38.5% as of December 31, showing a decrease of 20 basis points from the previous quarter.
