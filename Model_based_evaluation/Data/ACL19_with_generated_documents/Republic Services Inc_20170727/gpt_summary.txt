The report on the recent earnings conference call transcripts is as follows:

- Second quarter revenue was approximately $2.5 billion, an increase of $176 million or 7.5% over the prior year. This includes internal growth of 7.2% and acquisitions of 30 basis points.
- Average yield in the collection business was 3.1%, with core price increases of 4.1%.
- Total volume increased by 1.9% over the prior year.
- Fuel recovery fees increased by 60 basis points due to a rise in the cost of fuel.
- Energy services revenue increased by 70 basis points primarily due to an increase in drilling activity in the Permian Basin.
- Commodity sales revenue increased by 1.5% due to an increase in recycled commodity prices.
- Adjusted EBITDA margin was 28%, with a 40 basis point increase in landfill operating costs.
- Interest was $90 million, and the adjusted effective tax rate was 39.1%.
- Year-to-date adjusted free cash flow was $358 million, representing 6% growth over the prior year.
- Capital spending may need to increase to fund strong volume growth.
- Commodity prices increased by 35% to $157 per ton in the second quarter.
- EBITDA margins are expected to return to 30% with the abatement of temporary landfill operating costs.
- Adjusted cash flow guidance for the year remains double-digit when adjusted for cash taxes.
- CNG tax credits were not impactful as they went away this year.
- The business is experiencing strong revenue growth, good pricing, and the operating team is performing well.
- Seasonality is a factor to consider in the near term.

Based on the conference call transcripts, the company has experienced strong revenue growth and expects margin expansion with the abatement of temporary landfill operating costs. The strong performance in energy services revenue and commodity sales revenue indicates positive trends in the business. However, seasonal factors and the potential need to increase capital spending to fund growth are considerations for the remainder of the year. Overall, the company remains confident in its performance and outlook for the future.
