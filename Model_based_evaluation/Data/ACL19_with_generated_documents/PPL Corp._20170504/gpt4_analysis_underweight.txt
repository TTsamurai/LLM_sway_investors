**Future Outlook Report: Underweight Investment Stance on PPL Corporation**

**Introduction**:
In this report, we analyze PPL Corporation's (PPL) current financial health and future projections based on recent earnings conference call and financial statements. Our focus is on key financial metrics, operational performance, and strategic initiatives, leading to our investment stance recommendation.

**Current Financial Overview**:
PPL reported a decrease in earnings per share (EPS) from $0.71 in Q1 2016 to $0.59 in Q1 2017 on a reported basis, and from $0.67 to $0.62 on an adjusted basis. This decline was primarily attributed to unfavorable foreign exchange rates and lower energy consumption due to an unusually mild winter in Kentucky. Despite the earnings slump, PPL reaffirmed its 2017 EPS guidance at $2.05 to $2.25, indicating management confidence in achieving forecasted results.

**Operational Highlights**:
1. **UK Market and Brexit**: PPL's UK operations commenced discussions around incentives and operation stability post-Brexit, which has shown surprising steadiness in forex rates. Despite hedging strategies that mitigate forex risk, prolonged uncertainty and any adverse developments could impact future profitability.
   
2. **U.S. Regulatory Developments**: In the U.S., particularly Kentucky and Pennsylvania, regulatory and operational adjustments are ongoing. The Kentucky rate case, pending approval, might boost revenues. However, some capital expenditures have been deferred ($300 million), which could affect growth projections.

3. **Growth Projections and Strategic Initiatives**: PPL targets a 5-6% compound annual growth rate (CAGR) in EPS from 2017 through 2020, driven mainly by operations in both the U.S. and UK. Strategic efforts include enhancing utility infrastructure and focusing on sustainable and reliable energy provision. Furthermore, PPL is emphasizing organic growth over acquisitions, particularly focusing on capital deployment in the U.S.

**Risks and Concerns**:
1. **Exchange Rate Instability**: While currently stable, further fluctuations in exchange rates, particularly the pound to dollar, pose a risk. Ensuring hedging strategies efficiently mitigate these risks is critical.
2. **Regulatory Risks**: Ongoing and future regulatory decisions (e.g., Kentucky rate case) pose potential impacts on profitability and operational efficiency.
3. **Operational Execution**: Delays or inefficiencies in executing strategic plans, like the deferral in capital expenditures, could hinder projected growth.
4. **Brexit Related Uncertainty**: While initial impacts appear minimal, the long-term repercussions on PPL's UK operations remain uncertain.

**Conclusion and Recommendation**:

Investors should closely monitor forthcoming regulatory decisions and post-Brexit developments impacting operations in the UK.
