Key Insights:
- Weyerhaeuser reported strong financial performance in the first quarter, with net earnings of $157 million and net sales of $1.7 billion. Excluding after-tax special items, earnings were $167 million, marking a significant improvement over the previous quarter and the same period last year.
- Adjusted EBITDA for the first quarter was $454 million, representing a 14% increase compared to the fourth quarter and a 35% increase compared to the first quarter of 2016.
- The housing market showed signs of strength, with total housing starts averaging over $1.25 million for the first quarter, leading to robust demand for Weyerhaeuser's products.
- The Timberlands segment contributed $148 million to first quarter earnings, with strong performance in Western Timberlands and positive market conditions in export markets like Japan and China.
- Real Estate and Energy & Natural Resources contributed $26 million to first quarter earnings and $43 million to adjusted EBITDA, showing improvement compared to the same period last year.
- Wood Products contributed $172 million to first quarter earnings, with significant improvement in lumber, OSB, and engineered wood products. The Softwood Lumber Agreement and countervailing duties on Canadian lumber producers were also discussed in the context of the company's outlook.
