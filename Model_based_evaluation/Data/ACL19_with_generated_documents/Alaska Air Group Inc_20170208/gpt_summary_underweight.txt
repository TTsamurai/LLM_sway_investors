2. Earnings Per Share (EPS): Earnings per share rose by 12% to $7.32, indicating a positive trend in profitability.

3. Return on Invested Capital (ROIC): The after-tax ROIC was reported at 21.3%, which is a strong indicator of the company's capital efficiency.

4. Pre-tax Profit Improvement: The pre-tax profit for the quarter saw a $16 million improvement over the prior year, with Virgin America contributing $24 million to the pre-tax profit.

5. Revenue and Cost Trends: Unit revenue results were reported to be positive, while the CASM (cost per available seat mile) excluding fuel remained flat for the quarter. However, economic fuel costs per gallon were up year-over-year.

6. Cash Flow and Capital Expenditure: The company reported total cash flow from operations of $1.5 billion and CapEx of just under $680 million, resulting in free cash flow of over $820 million.

7. Debt and Dividend: The company ended the year with $2.6 billion of long-term debt, and the board announced an increase in the dividend by 9% to $0.30 per share per quarter, signaling confidence in the company's plan and ability to generate strong results.

Based on the above financial metrics, the company has shown positive profitability and capital efficiency, but there are potential challenges such as rising fuel costs and increased capital expenditure in 2017. As a result, the 'underweight' investment stance is recommended to reflect the potential risks associated with these challenges.
