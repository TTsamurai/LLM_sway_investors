Key financial metrics from the earnings conference call transcript include:
- First quarter revenue of $1.8 billion, representing a 12% increase driven by acquisitions and organic growth.
- EPS of $1.09 included a gain of $0.39, with adjusted EPS of $0.70 exceeding expectations due to strong performance and a lower tax rate.
- Adjusted segment margin was 11.8%, an 80 basis point improvement over last year, driven by strong incremental margin in the energy segment.
- Bookings increased 21% to $2 billion, with a broad based positive result reflecting organic and acquisition growth.
- Free cash flow for the first quarter was $36 million, impacted by inventory increases driven by selective pre-builds, with a full year commitment to free cash flow.
- Organic growth was strong in energies, refrigeration and food equipment, and engineered systems, but declined in fluids.
- Energy revenue increased 14% year over year and 11% sequentially, exceeding expectations driven by improvements in US oil and gas fundamentals.
- Engineered Systems revenue increased 5% overall, with solid growth in printing and identification and slight organic decline in fluids segment.
- Foods revenue increased 32%, primarily driven by acquisitions, with organic revenue declining 2%.
- Refrigeration and food equipment's revenue included 5% organic growth, with solid activity in glass door and refrigeration case product lines.
- Corporate expense and interest expense were in line with expectations, with a first quarter tax rate of 25.7%.
- Annual guidance for total revenue was increased to 11% to 13%, with organic revenue growth of 4% to 6%, and expected full year segment margin of around 14%.
- Expected 2017 EPS range is $4.05 to $4.20, with year over year margins expected to expand.

Additionally, there were discussions on Wayne margins, operating margins, conversion rates, inventory levels, and cash payments against reserves.
