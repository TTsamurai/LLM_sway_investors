**Investment Analysis and Recommendation: Overweight on Harris Corporation**

**Executive Summary:**
Harris Corporation (NYSE: HRS) has demonstrated robust fiscal performance and strategic initiatives that underscore its growth potential and operational excellence. The recent earnings call highlighted the company's strong execution across various segments and revealed key financial data, orders, and forward-looking guidance that position Harris favorably in the competitive aerospace and defense sector. Based on these dynamics, we recommend an ‘overweight’ investment stance on Harris Corporation.

**Financial Performance Highlights:**

1. **Revenue Growth and Segment Performance:**
   - **Communication Systems:** Revenue up 3% year-over-year with an impressive 22% increase in operating income and 500 basis point margin expansion, primarily driven by tactical radio volume growth and operational efficiencies.
   - **Public Safety and Professional Communications (PSPC):** Solid book-to-bill ratio despite a revenue dip, ensuring potential future growth driven by significant contracts, such as a $461 million IDIQ contract with the U.S. Army.
   - **Electronic Systems:** Showed strong year-on-year growth, bolstered by electronic warfare advancements and avionics, partially offsetting declines related to the ADS-B program transition.
   - **Space and Intelligence Systems:** Despite a slight revenue decrease, the segment experienced an 8% rise in operating income and expanded margins, reflecting effective program management and a good grasp on lucrative classified contracts.

2. **Strategic Orders and Growth Outlook:**
    - A landmark $260 million order for Australia’s Phase 3 modernization program and continued success in securing substantial international and domestic orders underline Harris’s competitive edge and strategic market positioning.
    - For FY 2018, Harris projects revenue to increase by 2% to 4% and EPS to grow by 6% to 9%, indicating confidence in sustained operational momentum and financial health.

3. **Operational Excellence and Cost Management:**
    - Harris continued to reap benefits from integration and operational excellence programs that enhanced margins across several key business segments. The expected operational efficiencies and increased pension income are poised to bolster the EPS in the upcoming fiscal year.

4. **Capital Allocation and Shareholder Returns:**
    - The company demonstrated prudent capital management strategies with plans for significant debt repayment, share repurchases, and consistent dividend payments, all of which enhance shareholder value.

**Investment Thesis:**
The comprehensive analysis of Harris Corporation’s performance and strategic direction indicates solid growth prospects pivoted on technological advancements, robust order backlog, and strategic capital allocation. Harris is well-positioned to capitalize on favorable defense spending trends, international growth opportunities, and technological innovations in communications, space, and electronic systems.

**Recommendation:**
Given the company's strong fiscal discipline, forward-looking growth strategy, and robust segment performance, we assign an 'overweight' rating. Harris Corporation presents a compelling investment opportunity for investors seeking exposure to a well-managed, growth-oriented aerospace and defense player with a clear path to continued value creation.

**Action Items:**
- Consider increasing stake in Harris Corporation based on the outlined financial strengths and strategic initiatives.
- Monitor quarterly performance for sustained revenue growth, margin improvements, and successful execution of outlined strategic orders.
- Stay informed on global defense spending trends that could impact Harris’s key market segments.

This strategic overview underscores our confidence in Harris Corporation’s ability to outperform and deliver substantial shareholder value, reinforcing our overweight investment stance.
