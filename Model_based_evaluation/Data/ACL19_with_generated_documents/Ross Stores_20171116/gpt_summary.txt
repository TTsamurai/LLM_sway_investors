Based on the conference call transcript, the key financial metrics for the third quarter and year-to-date performance are as follows:

1. Earnings per share for the third quarter were $0.72, up 16% from last year. For the first nine months of fiscal 2017, earnings per share were $2.36, up 15% on top of an 11% increase in the prior year.

2. Net earnings grew to $274 million in the third quarter compared to $204 million in the prior year. For the first nine months of fiscal 2017, net earnings were $912 million, up from $817 million last year.

3. Sales for the third quarter rose 8% to $3.3 billion, with comparable store sales up 4%. Year-to-date, sales increased by 8% to $10.1 billion, with comparable store sales up 4%.

4. Operating margin for the third quarter was 13.3%, better than expected mainly due to higher merchandise margins and leverage on above-plan sales.

5. Inventory levels increased by 4% for the third quarter, with average in-store inventories flat compared to the prior year.

6. Ross Stores opened 30 new locations in the third quarter, completing their 2017 store opening program. They expect to end the year with a net increase of 88 locations for fiscal 2017.

In terms of the product mix and performance by category, the company mentioned that their children's business was the best-performing area and that their non-apparel and home businesses performed slightly ahead of the chain average.

Further, with regards to the outlook for the fourth quarter and the holiday season, the company stated that they anticipate an uncertain external environment and a very promotional holiday season. Despite this, they expressed confidence in their ability to achieve solid growth in sales and earnings over the long term.

Overall, the company seems pleased with their performance and is optimistic about their prospects for the fourth quarter, despite facing challenges such as tough prior year comparisons and an uncertain external environment.
