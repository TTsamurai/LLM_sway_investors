Key Insights:
1. Revenue increased 26% year-over-year, with the highest quarterly revenue since 2011.
2. Gross margin increased to 35%, primarily driven by IP-related revenue and a richer mix from the Computing and Graphics segment.
3. Operating income improved to $155 million, a solid improvement from $70 million a year ago.
4. Adjusted EBITDA increased to $191 million compared to $103 million a year ago.
5. Computing and Graphics segment revenue increased 74% year-over-year, with operating income improving to $70 million from a loss of $66 million a year ago.
6. Enterprise, Embedded and Semi-Custom revenue was flat year-over-year, with operating income decreasing to $84 million from $136 million a year ago.
7. Cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities totaled $879 million, with long-term debt at $1.36 billion.
8. Free cash flow increased to $32 million from $20 million in the year-ago period.

Overall Analysis:
1. The company has shown strong financial performance and momentum in its new premium products.
2. The IP monetization efforts have had a positive impact on both segments, contributing to the increase in gross margin.
3. The company's long-term model is on track with the expectation of reaching a gross margin of greater than 36% in 2018.
4. Operating expenses are being targeted towards R&D investments to support the growth opportunities, with the expense to revenue ratio expected to be approximately 30% in 2017, at the upper end of the long-term target model of 26% to 30%.
5. The company is making investments to support the product roadmap and go-to-market costs for new products, while bringing down the percentage of OpEx over revenue.

Based on the strong financial performance, revenue growth, and positive outlook for gross margin expansion, it is recommended to adopt an 'overweight' investment stance for the company. The company's focus on innovation, strong product portfolio, and improving financial metrics suggest potential for long-term growth and profitability.
