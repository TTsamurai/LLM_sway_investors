Key financial metrics from the recent earnings conference call transcripts include:

1. Revenue: Garmin reported strong revenue growth for the quarter, with a 10% increase over the prior year to $861 million. For the full year, revenue increased 7% over 2015, exceeding $3 billion.

2. Segment Performance: Outdoor, Fitness, Marine, and Aviation collectively contributed 74% of total revenues, with strong growth in each segment. Outdoor revenue increased 33%, Fitness revenue grew by 24%, Marine revenue increased by 16%, and Aviation reported a 10% revenue growth.

3. Gross Margin: Garmin's gross margin improved to 54.7% for the quarter and 55.6% for the full year. The Marine segment had a steady gross margin at 55%.

4. Operating Margin: The operating margin was essentially flat at 18.6% for the quarter and improved to 20.7% for the full year. The Aviation segment maintained a strong operating margin of 28%.

5. Operating Income: Operating income increased 10% for the quarter and 14% for the full year. The Marine segment reported a significant 82% growth in operating income.

6. Earnings Per Share (EPS): Garmin reported GAAP EPS of $0.72 and pro forma EPS of $0.73 for the quarter. For the full year, GAAP EPS was $2.70 and pro forma EPS was $2.83.

7. Unit Deliveries: Unit deliveries increased by 4% to 16.8 million for the year.

8. Projections for 2017: Garmin anticipates revenue growth of approximately 10% in the Outdoor segment, 5% in the Fitness segment, 10% in the Marine segment, 5% in the Aviation segment, and a decline of approximately 17% in the Auto segment. The company is projecting a steady gross margin of approximately 56% and an operating margin of approximately 20%. The pro forma earnings per share is expected to be approximately $2.65, with a pro forma effective tax rate of approximately 22%.

These key financial metrics demonstrate strong performance across multiple segments, with revenue growth, improved margins, and increased operating income. The company's outlook for 2017 also indicates opportunities for growth despite challenges in certain segments.
