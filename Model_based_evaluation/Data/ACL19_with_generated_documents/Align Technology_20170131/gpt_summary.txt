Key financial metrics from the recent earnings conference call transcripts include:
- Total company revenue for the fourth quarter was $293.2 million, up 5.2% from the prior quarter and up 27.3% from the corresponding quarter a year ago.
- Fourth quarter clear aligner revenue of $251.5 million was up 3.2% sequentially, reflecting growth of Invisalign volume partially offset by lower Invisalign ASPs.
- Q4 gross margins for the scanner segment was a record 61%, up 3.9 point sequentially and 23.2 point year-over-year.
- Q4 operating margin was 23.3%, up one point sequentially and down two and a half point year-over-year.
- Cash flow from operations for the fourth quarter was $81 million and free cash flow for the quarter was a record $66.8 million.
- Full year 2016 revenue was a record $1.1 billion, up 27.7% year-over-year.
- Full year 2016 diluted EPS was $2.33. 
- In 2016, the company repurchased 1.1 million shares of Align stock for $96.2 million.
- For Q1 outlook, Invisalign case volume is anticipated to be in the range of 200,000 to 203,000 cases, an increase of approximately 22.2% to 24% over the same period a year ago.
- Q1 net revenues are expected to be in the range of $295 million to $298 million, an increase of 23.6% to 24.8% year-over-year.
- Q1 operating margin is expected to be in the range of 19.1% to 19.3%. 
- Q1 diluted earnings per share is expected to be in the range of $0.64 to $0.67.

Further, the company anticipates 2017 revenue growth to be above the midpoint of the long-term operating model range of 15% to 25%. Operating margins are expected to be flat to slightly up over 2016, and the equity loss from their investment in Smile Direct Club is expected to be two to three times the 2016 losses. The effective tax rate for 2017 is anticipated to be approximately 18%. The company also expects their earnings power in the second half of the year to be stronger than in the first half.
