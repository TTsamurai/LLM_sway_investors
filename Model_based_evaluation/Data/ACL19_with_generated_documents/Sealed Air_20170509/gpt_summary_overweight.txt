Additionally, the company increased its share repurchase program by an additional $1.5 billion, expanding its total authorization for future repurchases to approximately $2.2 billion.

In terms of first quarter results, Sealed Air reported a solid start to the year with sales of $1 billion, representing a 3% constant dollar growth. Both the Food Care and Product Care divisions showed positive performance, contributing to the company's overall sales growth, with North America demonstrating strong sales growth of 6%.

Furthermore, the company delivered adjusted EBITDA of $182 million, or 18% of sales, from continuing operations. The regional performance also showed positive trends, with strong volume growth in North America and positive volume trends in Latin America.

In Food Care, the division delivered 2% constant dollar sales growth, with a strong performance in North America and positive volume trends in Latin America. The company expects Food Care to continue delivering 3% constant sales growth for the full year 2017.

In Product Care, net sales increased by 4% in constant dollars, with all regions delivering positive constant dollar sales and volume growth. The company expects Product Care to continue experiencing constant dollar sales growth in the range of 3% to 4% for the full year 2017.

The company also addressed the impact of cost increases, including resin price increases, and unfavorable price/mix trends in the first quarter, highlighting that the negative impact is expected to improve in the second half of the year.

In conclusion, based on the key financial metrics and the company's strategic initiatives, such as the sale of Diversey and the performance of the Food Care and Product Care divisions, we recommend adopting an 'overweight' investment stance on Sealed Air Corporation. The company's strong performance, positive outlook, and focus on returning cash to shareholders position it well for future growth and value creation.
