Based on the recent quarterly earnings call from ADP, it is recommended to take an 'overweight' investment stance on the company. The first quarter fiscal 2018 results showed reported and organic revenue up 6% to $3.1 billion, and adjusted diluted earnings per share grew 6% to $0.91 per share. Additionally, new business bookings are expected to gradually expand back to pre-ACA growth levels, with an anticipated full year fiscal 2018 new business bookings growth of 5% to 7%. The company also saw a 160 basis point improvement in client retention and made significant progress in rationalizing its footprint by exiting nine subscale facilities. The company's innovations in HCM have been recognized in the industry, contributing to happier clients and improvement in Net Promoter Scores. ADP's acquisition of Global Cash Card is strengthening its core capabilities in digital payments and is expected to enhance its position in the payroll industry. The company has also made significant investments in innovation and technology, which are expected to drive sustained growth in the years ahead. These investments are aimed at delivering next-gen payroll and tax filing engines, as well as a low code application development platform, which will enable internal and external development teams to build agile country, segment, and client-specific applications. The company's management has expressed confidence in the future and the dedication of its associates in delivering valuable services to clients. Overall, the company has shown strong performance and is well-positioned for future growth, making it an attractive investment opportunity.
