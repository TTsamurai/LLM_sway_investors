Based on the information from the earnings conference call transcripts, here is a summary of the key financial metrics:

- Second quarter adjusted EPS was $0.41 per share, down 4.7% from 2016
- Reported revenues were up 2.3% to $898 million
- Organic sales growth was 1.8%, at the high end of the 1% to 2% range
- Consumer Domestic business' organic sales were flat, impacted by couponing and promotional investments
- International organic growth was up 7.4%
- Specialty Products division saw organic sales up 9.4%
- Adjusted second quarter gross margin was 45.7%, 80-basis point decrease from a year ago
- Marketing as a percent of revenue was 14.6%
- Operating margin was 18.1%, 190 basis points lower than the prior year
- Other expense was $6.4 million, primarily driven by interest expense
- Effective tax rate for the quarter was 33.1% on an adjusted basis
- Generated approximately $250 million of net cash for the first half
- Expect Q3 organic sales growth of 3.5%
- Expect Q3 earnings per share of approximately $0.46, a 2% decrease year-over-year
- Maintaining expectations for full-year organic sales growth of 3%
- Full-year EPS expected to be $1.79, excluding any impact from Water Pik
- Expect approximately $605 million of free cash flow for the full year

In conclusion, the company expects strong earnings growth in Q4, with continued focus on innovation support and marketing. The full-year expectations for organic sales growth, gross margin improvement, marketing expenses, operating margin expansion, other expenses, and EPS were outlined. The company also highlighted that they were able to beat their original expectations for settlement costs related to the UK pension settlement.
