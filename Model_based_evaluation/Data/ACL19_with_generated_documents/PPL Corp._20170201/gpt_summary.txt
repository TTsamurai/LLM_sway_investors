Here is a summary of the key financial metrics from the recent earnings conference call transcripts for PPL:

- PPL delivered strong 2016 ongoing earnings, achieving 11% year-over-year growth. The reported earnings for 2016 were $2.79 per share, compared with $1.01 per share in 2015.
- The company has raised its dividend by 4% for 2017, with a commitment to grow it by 4% annually through 2020.
- The Kentucky rate review is proceeding as expected, with proposed revenue increases driven by additional capital investments to improve the grid.
- PPL Electric Utilities has made substantial progress in transmission and distribution system improvements, resulting in a robust and advanced distribution automation system and improved reliability performance.
- In the UK, PPL received the highest incentive revenues among the UK's distribution network operators and has achieved a high customer satisfaction rating.
- The 2017 earnings forecast is reaffirmed at $2.05 to $2.25 per share, with a midpoint of $2.15.
- The company's capital plan of about $3 billion per year is expected to produce compound annual rate base growth of 5.3% from 2017 through 2020.
- PPL expects to achieve a competitive total annual shareholder return of 9% to 10% as it executes its growth plans.
- The company has minimal exposure to potential U.S. tax reform, with only half of its earnings subject to potential impacts, and has strategies in place to mitigate any negative effects.
- The company is confident in its ability to deliver on its projected 5% to 6% compound annual earnings growth from 2017 through 2020.

These key financial metrics demonstrate PPL's strong performance, strategic investments, and proactive risk management strategies. The company remains committed to delivering shareholder value and maintaining a competitive position in its industry.
