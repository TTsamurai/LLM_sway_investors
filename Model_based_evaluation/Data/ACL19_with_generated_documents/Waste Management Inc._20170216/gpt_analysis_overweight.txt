This reflects effective cost management and potential margin improvement.

4. Shareholder Value: The company remained committed to returning value to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases, totaling $1.45 billion during 2016. This demonstrates a shareholder-friendly approach and a focus on delivering value to investors.

5. Debt and Financial Position: The company maintained a strong balance sheet, with a debt-to-EBITDA ratio of 2.53 at the end of the fourth quarter. The weighted-average cost of debt was 4.17%, and the company expects interest expense and cash interest paid to be relatively flat with 2016. This indicates a solid financial position and manageable debt levels.

6. Revenue and Outlook: The company expects revenues to be just north of $14 billion in 2017, with a focus on volume growth, price increases, and operational efficiency. The company's strategic outlook for 2017 includes a target of $100 million to $200 million of tuck-in acquisitions, reflecting a growth-oriented strategy.

These factors support an 'overweight' investment stance, suggesting that the company has the potential for continued growth and value creation.
