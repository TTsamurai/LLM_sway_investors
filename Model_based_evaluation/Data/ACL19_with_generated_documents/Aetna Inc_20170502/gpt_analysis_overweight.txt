The Government medical benefit ratio was 85.3%, continuing strong results from 2016.

In the Commercial business, the company reported strong momentum, with Commercial ASC membership increasing by approximately 219,000 members in the quarter. The Commercial medical benefit ratio was 79.4%, indicating a very good result despite pressure from individual Commercial products. The company expects 2017 losses on individual Commercial products to be significantly less than those reported in 2016.

From a balance sheet perspective, the company's financial position, capital structure, and liquidity remain very strong, with a debt to total capitalization ratio of approximately 39.8% and approximately $100 million of cash at the parent at the end of the quarter.

Based on the first quarter performance, the company has increased its 2017 adjusted earnings per share guidance to a range of $8.80 to $9 per share, reflecting a meaningful increase despite a headwind from individual Commercial products. The company is also increasing its adjusted revenue projection for the year to approximately $61 billion and projects a full-year total healthcare medical benefit ratio in the range of 84%, plus or minus 50 basis points. Adjusted expense ratio is projected to be approximately 16.9% for the full year.
