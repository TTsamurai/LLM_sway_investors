In summary, the company has shown solid business performance in the first quarter, with strategic progress continuing and on track with the full-year outlook. The company reported flat or increased revenues of $1.3 billion compared to the prior-year period. Despite the impact of currency translation, revenue grew 3% on a constant currency basis. Revenue growth was driven by strong performance in westernunion. com and regions such as Latin America, North America, and Europe and CIS. Total C2C cross-border principal increased by 1% or 2% on a constant currency basis.
The company’s Consumer-to-Business segment also showed healthy growth, with revenue increasing 8% or 10% on a constant currency basis. Additionally, the Business Solutions revenue declined by 6% or 3% on a constant currency basis, affected by the loss of XE. com business and reduced sales of hedging products. Despite these declines, the company continues to expect savings from the WU Way transformation program, with approximately $20 million in savings included in the 2017 outlook. The company also expects an improvement in foreign exchange impact and a return to normal marketing spending, which should contribute to a stable operating margin.
The company’s outlook for the full year remains unchanged, with expected GAAP revenue to be flat to down low-single digits and adjusted operating margin of approximately 20%, excluding WU Way-related expenses. Additionally, the company projects savings of approximately $25 million in 2018 and expects other discrete tax benefits in the fourth quarter, resulting in a very low tax rate in Q4. The company continues to expect full-year GAAP earnings per share in the range of $1.48 to $1.60 and adjusted earnings per share, excluding WU Way-related expenses, in the range of $1.63 to $1.75.
Overall, with solid business performance, stable pricing environment, strategic initiatives, and optimistic outlook, an 'overweight' investment stance on the company is recommended, as there is potential for growth and improved performance in the coming quarters.
