Based on the earnings conference call transcripts, here is a detailed summary of key financial metrics for Sherwin-Williams in the first quarter of 2017:

1. Revenue and Profit: The first quarter of 2017 was strong for Sherwin-Williams from both a revenue and profit perspective. However, the Consumer segment had a bad quarter, with weak sales across most product categories and market segments.

2. Paint Stores Group: The Paint Stores Group saw positive momentum, with sales to residential repaint contractors continuing its double-digit growth trajectory, as well as sales to new residential painters, property management contractors, and commercial contractors. Protective and marine coating sales and volume also turned positive in the quarter.

3. Global Finishes Group: Solid top-line growth was achieved, with revenues up across all businesses and volumes up in most. The operating margin for this group was a cycle high of 11.1% in the first quarter.

4. Earnings per Share (EPS): Adjusted net income would have been $214.1 million, an increase of approximately 25%, and earnings per share would have been $2.27 per share, up 25.4%.

5. Working Capital: Net working capital increased slightly year-over-year as a percent of sales to 11.5% compared to 11.2% first quarter last year. Despite the higher working capital, the company generated $231.8 million in net operating cash, an improvement of more than $290 million compared to the first quarter of 2016.

6. Capital Expenditures: In 2017, the company anticipated capital expenditures of approximately $230 million, depreciation of $175 million to $185 million, and the amortization of about $30 million.

7. Dividend: The quarterly dividend approved by the Board of Directors was $0.85 per share compared to $0.84 per share last year.

8. Outlook for 2017: The company expects consolidated net sales to increase over 2016 by a mid-single-digit percentage. For the full year 2017, the updated full-year guidance is in the range of $13.65 to $13.85 per share compared to $11.99 per share in 2016.

Overall, the company is confident in its outlook and has plans in place to sustain its momentum throughout the year. However, it prefers to wait until the second quarter is complete before making any adjustments to its full-year guidance. The company aims to be the best partner possible to its customers, drive sell-through, and support their success.
