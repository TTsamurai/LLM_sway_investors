These are the key financial metrics from the recent earnings conference call transcripts:

1. Total Revenue: $9.9 billion, includes $2.5 billion for two months of enterprise services.
2. Revenue from continuing operations: $7.4 billion, down 5% adjusted for divestitures and currency.
3. Gross Margin: 33.8%, down 200 basis points year-over-year and down 240 basis points sequentially.
4. Non-GAAP Operating Profit: 7.8%, down 130 basis points year-over-year and down 270 basis points sequentially.
5. Non-GAAP Diluted Net Earnings Per Share: $0.35, includes non-GAAP diluted net earnings per share from continuing operations was $0.25 and $0.10 was from discontinued operations.

Performance by Business Segment:
1. Enterprise Group:
- Revenue was down 7%, but grew slightly year-over-year when adjusted for tier 1 sales.
- Operating margins were down 300 basis points year-over-year and 390 basis points sequentially to 8.8%.

2. Technology Services:
- Revenue up 3%, orders grew year-over-year for the fourth consecutive quarter.
- Operating profit declined 40 basis points year-over-year to 8.9%.

3. Software:
- Revenue was down 9% as declines in license and professional services more than offset growth in SaaS.
- Operating margin of 26.4% improved by 160 basis points year-over-year.

Cash Flow:
- Free cash flow was better than expected at negative $64 million.

Capital Allocation:
- $1.5 billion returned to shareholders in the first half of fiscal year '17 through dividend payments and share repurchases.

Outlook:
- Non-GAAP diluted net earnings per share outlook for fiscal year '17 maintained at $1.46 to $1.56.

Cost Management:
- Incremental $200 million to 300 million in cost savings expected in the second half of the year.
- Significant steps taken to optimize the cost structure with a focus on reducing spending and simplifying the organization.

Challenges and Mitigations:
- Elevated commodities cost in DRAM and year-over-year headwind from currency have impacted margins but are expected to be mitigated.
- Short-term dilution from recent acquisitions being addressed to ensure accretion in fiscal year '18.
- Margins negatively impacted by structural changes, stranded costs, short-term dilution from acquisitions, and competitive pricing environment.

Progress and Future Strategies:
- Committed to organic growth, share buybacks, and strategic M&A within a returns-based framework.
- Expect stabilization and return to growth in high-margin businesses.
- Supply constraints expected to loosen towards the end of the year.
- Employment of cost-saving measures and restructuring to optimize cost structure.

Overall, the company faced challenges in Q2 but is focused on addressing cost pressures, driving topline growth, and positioning for long-term success. The outlook for the fiscal year '17 remains positive, with ongoing efforts to stabilize margins and improve financial performance.
