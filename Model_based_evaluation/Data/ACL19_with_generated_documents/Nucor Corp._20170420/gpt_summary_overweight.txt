Key Financial Metrics:
- Earnings per diluted share: Nucor reported first quarter 2017 earnings of $1.11 per diluted share, representing a significant improvement over previous quarters. This is in line with the guidance range provided by the company. 
- Debt and liquidity: Nucor's total debt outstanding is $4.4 billion, with a gross debt-to-capital ratio of 34% at the end of the first quarter. The company has cash and short-term investments totaling approximately $1.7 billion, and an undrawn $1.5 billion unsecured revolving credit facility.
- Capital spending and depreciation: Nucor estimates capital spending of approximately $550 million and depreciation and amortization of about $730 million for 2017.
- Trade case decisions and imports: While recent trade case decisions have begun to reduce the flood of dumped and subsidized products from foreign producers, imports remain at a high 25% share of the U.S. steel industry. The company continues to address this issue and work towards effective enforcement of U.S. trade laws.
- Outlook for the second quarter: Nucor expects earnings in the second quarter of 2017 to increase compared to the first quarter. This is supported by expected improvements in price realizations, trade actions providing a more level playing field, and stronger demand in end-use markets such as nonresidential construction, energy, and heavy equipment.
