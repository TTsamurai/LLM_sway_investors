Based on the Q3 earnings conference call transcripts, the key financial metrics for the company are as follows:

1. Revenue: Q3 revenue was $1.6 billion, representing a 7% increase from the previous year, with approximately $40 million attributed to acquisitions. Organic revenue also saw a 5% increase.

2. Operating Earnings: GAAP operating earnings were $338 million, down $3 million from last year, while non-GAAP operating earnings were $423 million, up 7% from the prior year.

3. Earnings Per Share (EPS): GAAP EPS was $1.25, up 11% from the previous year, and non-GAAP EPS was $1.53, up 12% from the prior year.

4. Backlog: Ending backlog was $8.9 billion, a $768 million increase from last year, with product backlog up $344 million and services backlog up $424 million.

5. Product Sales: Q3 product sales were $989 million, showing an 8% increase from last year.

6. Operating Expenses: Total operating expenses were $375 million, up $5 million from the previous year, including $12 million related to acquisitions.

7. Cash Flow: Q3 operating cash flow was $270 million, a decrease of $78 million from last year, and free cash flow was $185 million, a decrease of $95 million from the previous year.

8. Dividends and Stock Repurchase: The company repurchased $100 million of stock and paid dividends of $76 million during the quarter.

9. Outlook: The company raised its Q4 and full-year guidance, expecting Q4 revenue growth of approximately 3% and non-GAAP EPS between $2 and $2.05. The full-year 2017 revenue growth is expected to be approximately 5% with non-GAAP EPS between $5.35 and $5.40.

10. Regional Results: Americas revenue was up 9%, EMEA was up 6%, and Asia Pac was up 1%.

Based on these financial metrics, the company has shown strong revenue growth, backed by increases in product sales and backlog. The company also experienced higher operating expenses, leading to a decrease in cash flow compared to the previous year. However, the company's outlook for Q4 and full-year 2017 is positive, with an increase in revenue guidance and non-GAAP EPS. Notable segment highlights include winning several significant deals in the products segment and securing new Managed & Support contracts in the services segment. Additionally, the company closed the acquisition of Kodiak Networks, expanding its push-to-talk over cellular solutions.
