Based on the recent earnings conference call transcripts of Cognizant, the following key financial metrics can be summarized:

1. Q3 Revenue: The third quarter revenue was $3.77 billion, which is at the high end of their guided range and up 9.1% year-over-year.

2. Business Segment Performance: Three of the four business segments (Healthcare, Product and Resources, and Communications and Media and Technology) averaged double-digit growth rates. Digital related revenue grew well above company average.

3. Non-GAAP EPS and Operating Margin: The non-GAAP EPS for the quarter was $0.98 and the non-GAAP operating margin was 20%.

4. Full-Year Revenue Guidance: The low end of the revenue guidance range was raised, expecting the full-year revenue to be in the range of $14.78 billion and $14.84 billion, with a full-year 2017 non-GAAP operating margin at least 19.6%.

5. Digital Transformation and Acquisitions: Cognizant has been systematically building out significant capabilities to enable clients to transition from physical to digital world. They recently announced two acquisitions, Netcentric and Zone, broadening their portfolio of digital services and solutions.

6. Digital Revenue and Margin Improvement: The company reported that digital revenue grew at a double-digit pace, ahead of company average. They highlighted that the portfolio of digital revenue is running at a higher margin than the rest of the business.

7. Client Examples of Digital at Scale: The transcript included examples of how Cognizant has helped clients in the automotive, financial, and life sciences industries to implement digital solutions that deliver business outcomes and competitive upside for the company.

8. Organizational Capability and Expertise: Cognizant has been aggressively building high-end digital skills in areas such as data science, design thinking, cybersecurity, the Internet of Things, artificial intelligence, and automation, and combining these skills with industry expertise.

9. Practice Areas and Revenue Synergies: The company has three practice areas: Cognizant Digital Business, Cognizant Digital Operations, and Cognizant Digital Systems and Technology. They are focusing on running the acquisitions independently and executing on revenue synergies by integrating and cross-selling their traditional services.

10. Repeat Business and Client Relevance: Cognizant tracks repeat business from their existing clients as a metric to assess their relevance and innovation, aiming to continue to be relevant to their existing clients by finding new sources of value.

Overall, the company is focused on digital at scale, creating repeatable solutions, and driving revenue growth and margin improvement through their digital services and capabilities.
