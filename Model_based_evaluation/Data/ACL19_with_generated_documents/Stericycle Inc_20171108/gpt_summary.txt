Key financial metrics from the earnings conference call transcripts include:

- Global revenues were $882.8 million, a decrease of 0.8% from Q3 2016. Organic revenue, adjusted for various factors, increased by 0.2%.
- Domestic and Canada revenues were $708.2 million, with organic revenue decreasing by 0.2%.
- International revenues were $174.6 million, with organic revenue decreasing by 2.2%.
- Gross profit was $368 million, or 41.7% of revenues.
- Adjusted SG&A, excluding amortization, was $192.2 million, or 21.8% of revenues.
- Adjusted income from operations or EBITDA was $175.9 million, or 19.9% of revenues.
- Net interest expense was $24.5 million.
- Net income attributable to Stericycle was $35.4 million on an as-reported basis and $1.10 when adjusted for certain expenses.
- Covenant debt to EBITDA ratio was 3.48 at the end of the quarter.
- Unused portion of the revolver was approximately $712 million.
- Year-to-date cash from operations was $392 million on an as-reported basis and $462.1 million when adjusted.
- Year-to-date CapEx was $91.7 million, or 3.4% of revenues.
- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) was 64 days.
- The company is in the process of renewing and extending its existing senior credit facility and term loans.
- The revised Q4 EPS guidance ranges from $1.12 to $1.18, reflecting various factors such as M&I volume, regulated waste and compliance services revenue, hurricane impacts, international business impacts, and other financial adjustments.
- Earnings guidance for 2018 was stated to be flat compared to 2017, with more detailed guidance expected in the Q4 call in February.
- Market competitiveness and pricing strategies were discussed, indicating a competitive market and potential impact on project pricing.
- Foreign exchange favorability and impact of acquisitions on revenue were stated to be around $8 million and $1 million, respectively.
- The EBITDA margins and profit improvement efforts were discussed, highlighting revenue shortfalls, hurricane impacts, and business transformation savings and costs.
- The company mentioned that they have been operating towards the mid to low end of their financial ranges, leading to adjustments in the top end of the range for Q4.

These key financial metrics provide an overview of the company's performance, revenue breakdown, profit margins, future earnings guidance, and market competitiveness.
