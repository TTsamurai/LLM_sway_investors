 based on the recent earnings conference call transcripts, here is a summary of the key financial metrics:

Bookings
- Bookings in the first quarter were $5.7 billion, exceeding expectations and down slightly from the same period last year.
- The total book-to-bill ratio is 1.12 on a trailing four-quarter basis.
- Approximately 33% of total bookings were from international awards, representing an increase of approximately 14% over last year's first quarter.
- Backlog at the end of the first quarter was $36.1 billion, an increase of approximately $1.9 billion or 5.5% compared to the first quarter of 2016. Around 41% of the backlog comprised of international programs.

Sales
- Sales in the first quarter were $6 billion, up 3.4%, with particularly strong performance in IDS and SAS businesses.
- Net sales by business segment: IDS - $1.4 billion, IIS - $1.5 billion, Missile Systems - $1.8 billion, SAS - $1.6 billion, Forcepoint - up by 4%.

Margins
- Operating margin was 12.4% for the total company, an increase of 180 basis points.
- Business segment operating margin was 11.8%, up by 160 basis points.
- Margin improvement was driven by favorable program mix and some of the largest international awards progressing through their lifecycle.

Earnings Per Share (EPS)
- First quarter 2017 EPS from continuing operations was $1.73, up 21% from last year's first quarter and higher than expected.

Financial Outlook
- Full year 2017 net sales range increased by $100 million, now in the range of $24.9 billion to $25.4 billion.
- Full year EPS expected to be in the range of $7.25 to $7.40.
- Full year effective tax rate approximately 31%.
- Expected full year operating cash flow between $2.8 billion and $3.1 billion.

Capital Deployment
- It is expected to return approximately 80% of free cash flow to shareholders.
- Raised dividend by 8.9% for the 13th year in a row.
- Expected share buyback to approximate 2016's levels.

Overall, the company achieved strong performance in the first quarter, exceeding expectations in bookings, sales, and EPS. The company's balanced capital deployment strategy and strong financial outlook indicate a positive future performance.
