Based on the recent earnings conference call transcripts, the key financial metrics for Kroger include the following:

- Identical food store sales: For the second quarter-to-date, the identical food store sales were positive, showing growth in sales.

- Tonnage: Continued to be positive in the first quarter, contributing to sales growth.

- Visits per household: Remained flat in the first quarter, indicating stable customer traffic.

- Gross margin: Declined in the first quarter, impacting profitability. Operating costs were also up, resulting in a decrease in FIFO operating profit.

- Digital revenue: More than doubled in the first quarter, reflecting the growing digital business for Kroger.

- Debt to adjusted EBITDA ratio: Increased to 2.33 times compared to the same period last year, due to the merger with ModernHEALTH and share repurchases.

- Return on invested capital: 12.75% for the first quarter on a rolling four-quarter basis, indicating the return on capital investments.

- Labor negotiations: Currently negotiating agreements with UFCW for store associates in certain regions, with the aim of providing competitive compensation packages.

- Guidance for fiscal 2017: Revised net earnings per diluted share guidance to $1.74 to $1.79, with adjusted net earnings guidance range of $2 to $2.05. Identical supermarket sales, excluding fuel, expected to grow at a rate of flat to 1% for 2017.

- Capital investments: Expected to be in the range of $3.2 billion to $3.5 billion for 2017.

Overall, the company is focused on managing costs, improving profitability, and investing in growth areas such as digital revenue and labor relations to support long-term financial sustainability and performance.
