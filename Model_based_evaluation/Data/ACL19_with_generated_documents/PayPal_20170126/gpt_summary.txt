Key Financial Metrics:
- PayPal reported $0.42 of non-GAAP EPS at the top end of their non-GAAP guidance of $0.42
- $2.981 billion in revenues, an increase of 19% on an FX neutral basis
- $771 million in free cash flow
- Full year non-GAAP EPS of $1.50, above initial guidance of $1.45 to $1.50
- Revenues grew 21% on a pro forma FX neutral basis above initial 2016 guidance
- $10.84 billion of revenue and $2.5 billion in free cash flow for the full year
- Ended the year with 197 million active customer accounts, adding 5.4 million new accounts in Q4
- Customer engagement in Q4 increased to 31 transactions for active accounts
- 25% increase in payment volume, resulting in $99.3 billion of payment volume in the quarter
- Mobile accounted for a third of overall payment volume during the quarter
- 6 billion payments processed in 2016, representing a total payment volume of $354 billion
- Venmo processed 5.6 billion volume in Q4, up 126% and processed $17.6 billion for the full year, up 135%
- Ended the year with over 5 million merchants offering One Touch to more than 40 million consumers
- Expanded focus on partnerships with financial institutions, mobile carriers, and leading consumer brands
- Secular tailwinds and strong performance driving increased confidence in top line growth
- Mobile driving growth and serving as a competitive differentiator
- Plans to rollout Venmo monetization and customer choice in 2017, with impact expected in 2018 and 2019
- Encouraging initial results from the rollout of customer choice, leading to increased engagement and higher basket sizes.
