. As per the conference call transcript, Chevron's first quarter 2017 earnings were $2.7 billion or $1.41 per diluted share. Excluding foreign exchange and special items, earnings for the quarter totaled $2.3 billion or $1.23 per share. Cash from operations for the quarter was $3.9 billion and included about $1 billion in working capital consumption. Excluding working capital, cash flow from operations was $4.8 billion. At quarter end, debt balances stood at $45 billion, with a debt ratio of approximately 24%. On a net debt basis, our debt net of cash totaled $38 billion. First quarter cash capital spend was $3.3 billion, approximately 40% less than the first quarter of 2016. Asset sale proceeds were $2.1 billion, primarily from the sale of geothermal assets in Indonesia. 

Results for the first quarter of 2017 were $3.4 billion higher than first quarter 2016 results. Special items, primarily a gain from the sale of Indonesian geothermal assets, increased earnings by $795 million between periods. Upstream and downstream earnings also saw increases.

During the call, leaders discussed a focus on improving returns, reducing spend, and unlocking value from the portfolio. They indicated a focus on maintaining and growing the dividend as earnings and cash flow permit.

The company intends to be cash balanced in 2017 at $50 Brent prices through growing operating cash flow, reduced capital spend, and proceeds from asset sales. However, uncertainties such as project startups, external events, and price and spend levels may impact production growth for 2017.

In response to questions, the company mentioned ongoing negotiations and discussions regarding planned projects and asset sales. They also acknowledged the volatile nature of corporate sector expenses and variable corporate tax entries.

Overall, the company seems to be focused on financial priorities and cost reduction measures to improve their financial metrics. These efforts, along with asset sales and improved revenue, have contributed to a positive first quarter performance.
