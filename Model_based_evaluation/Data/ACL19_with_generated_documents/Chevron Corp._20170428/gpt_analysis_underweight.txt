The company anticipates additional growth from various projects and assets, which should support accelerated production through the year. Additionally, Chevron has been focused on lowering its cost structure and reducing capital spend. Quarter-to-date capital expenditures of $4.4 billion are down 22% compared to the average 2016 quarter and down 56% compared to the average 2014 quarter.

The company has also achieved significant progress in asset sales, with approximately $5 billion in assets sold since the beginning of 2016. Additionally, Chevron aims to be cash balanced in 2017 at $50 Brent prices and to continue to grow free cash flow thereafter.

In terms of financial priorities, Chevron aims to maintain and grow its dividend, improve returns, and unlock value from its portfolio. The company is focused on taking actions to enable cash balance, improving returns as projects are completed and revenue is realized, and unlocking value from its entire portfolio.
