**Key Financial Highlights Q1 2017:**

1. **Revenue Growth:**
   - Total revenue growth was reported at 25.1% year-over-year (YoY), driven by acquisitions and robust internal storage revenue growth of 3%, excluding impacts from Recall and other smaller acquisitions.
   - Service revenues showed a notable increase of 26.6% in the same period, with 0.6% internal growth derived from higher paper prices and increases in project-related revenue.

2. **Adjusted EBITDA:**
   - Adjusted EBITDA growth rose by 24.4%, bolstered by the benefits from acquisitions and improved operational efficiencies. 
   - Adjusted EBITDA margin stood at 31.2%, experiencing a marginal decline of 10 basis points YoY due to the integration of lower-margin Recall business.

3. **Adjusted Gross Margin and SG&A:**
   - The adjusted gross margin declined by 120 basis points YoY impacted by the mix of Recall's lower margins.
   - SG&A expenses were up by 20.1% YoY but decreased as a percent of total revenue by 100 basis points thanks to integration benefits and reductions in bad debt expenses.

4. **Net Earnings and EPS:**
   - Adjusted EPS was $0.24 per diluted share, influenced by a negative $0.03 per share due to an increase in the structural tax rate from 14% to 23.1% YoY.
   
5. **Cash Metrics:**
   - Adjusted Funds From Operations (AFFO) was reported at $170.9 million, in line with expectations despite a $34 million increase in cash taxes paid YoY.

6. **Balance Sheet and Liquidity:**
   - Liquidity stood at nearly $1 billion at quarter-end with a lease-adjusted debt ratio of 5.8 times, anticipated to decrease to 5 times by 2020.

7. **Guidance for FY 2017:**
   - The company forecasts an adjusted EBITDA growth of 17.5% and an EBITDA margin expansion of about 200 basis points, with adjusted EBITDA margins potentially reaching around 33%.
   - AFFO is expected to grow by 11.5% at the midpoint, supporting a projected dividend growth of 7% in 2018.

**Segment Performance:**
- **North America**: Both records and information management (RIM) and data management showed strong internal storage revenue growth, although service revenue from data management saw a decline.
- **Western Europe**: Experienced an improvement in both internal storage and service revenue growth.
- **Other International**: Continued robust growth was seen, particularly in the legacy Recall Australian business.

**Strategic Focus:**
- The company remains focused on integrating Recall effectively, reducing overall cost structures, and optimizing profitability. Innovative projects and enhancements in service offerings are ongoing to adapt to market demands and enhance customer satisfaction.

**Conclusion:**
The company demonstrated strong performance amidst integration challenges and changing market dynamics. Revenue growth, driven by strategic acquisitions and organic growth initiatives, underpinned the company's robust financial health. Looking ahead, the company is well-positioned to meet its financial goals for 2017, backed by effective cost management and strategic innovation investments.
