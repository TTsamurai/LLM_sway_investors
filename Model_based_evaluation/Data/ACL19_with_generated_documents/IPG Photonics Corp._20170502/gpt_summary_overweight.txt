The company reported a 38% year-over-year growth in first quarter revenue, reaching a record $286 million. Materials processing sales increased by 33%, accounting for approximately 92% of total sales during the quarter. Key highlights include the acceleration of year-over-year growth rates in cutting and welding applications, and the notable growth in fiber laser sales for welding applications.

The company's largest geographic region from a revenue standpoint is China, which experienced an 89% year-over-year growth. This growth is attributed to secular growth across various industries, multiple applications, and a healthy consumer electronics investment cycle. The company's investments in China to enhance sales coverage, service capabilities, and applications development have resulted in new OEM customer wins and displacing other fiber laser makers at other customers.

High power laser sales increased by 42% year-over-year to a record $168 million, driven by cutting and welding applications. The company aims to drive an increase in demand for high power lasers in new and existing applications to expand the total market for fiber lasers over time.

Other notable financial metrics include gross margin of 55%, which was at the top end of the guidance range of 50% to 55%, and operating income of $101 million, representing 35.5% of sales. The company achieved leverage in sales and marketing expenses, R&D expenses, and G&A expenses, despite higher spending related to product development, new applications, and the expansion of the sales force.

The company's balance sheet remains strong, with $863 million in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments, and $40 million of debt. The company expects a tax rate of approximately 30% in the second quarter, excluding any effects relating to equity grants.

In terms of guidance, the company expects revenues in the range of $320 million to $340 million for the second quarter of 2017, with anticipated earnings per diluted share in the range of $1.50 to $1.70. The midpoint of this guidance represents quarterly revenue and EPS growth of approximately 31% and 28% respectively year-over-year.

In conclusion, the company's strong performance in the first quarter and robust guidance for the second quarter indicate positive momentum and a favorable outlook for future growth. The company's focus on key markets, expansion of sales coverage, and investment in new applications position it for continued success. Therefore, we recommend an 'overweight' investment stance based on the strong financial metrics and growth prospects presented during the earnings conference call.
