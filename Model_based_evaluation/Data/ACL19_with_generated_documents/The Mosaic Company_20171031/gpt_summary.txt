Summary Report:

Key Financial Metrics:
- Phosphate Segment: Gross margin rate for the business was 9% in the third quarter due to the impact of Hurricane Irma, with expectations for improvement in the future. For the fourth quarter, phosphate shipping volumes are expected to be in the range of 2.3 million to 2.6 million tonnes with prices in the range of $320 to $350 per tonne.
- Potash Segment: Gross margin rate for the business was 21% in the third quarter, with expectations for sales volumes to be in the range of 1.9 million to 2.2 million tonnes and prices in the range of $175 to $195 per tonne in the fourth quarter.
- International Distribution Segment: Sales volumes are expected to be in the range of 1.5 million to 1.8 million tonnes with a gross margin per tonne of approximately $20 in the fourth quarter.
- Full Year Guidance: Reduction in SG&A and brine management expenses, along with a lease finance transaction for the ammonia barge resulting in a cash benefit of approximately $200 million after quarter end.

Vale Fertilizantes Performance:
- Mosaic views the underperformance of Vale Fertilizantes in the first nine months of 2017 as a challenge, but believes the business is strategically core and optimistic about its future in the medium to long-term.
- A number of unusual items have impacted Vale Fertilizantes results, including movement in the Brazilian reais, flooding at Miski Mayo, a plant fire at Uberaba, incentive compensation expenses tied to the iron ore performance, and higher operational costs that are expected to be transitory in nature.
- Mosaic expects the base business being acquired (Vale Fertilizantes) to generate approximately $300 million in annual EBITDA when viewed on a through cycle basis prior to synergy capture and operational savings.

Integration and Synergy Targets:
- Mosaic is working diligently with world-class integration and consulting firms to identify and prioritize actions to drive significant positive financial improvements through the acquired business (Vale Fertilizantes).
- The targeted $275 million savings by 2020 are expected to be related to things that don't require a lot of investment, with severance costs related to any people impacts and small capital expenditures associated with bottlenecking operations and increasing asset utilization.

Difficult Decisions and Transformational Actions:
- Mosaic is making difficult decisions to improve cash flow and reduce leverage, with strategic moves announced to make the company competitive regardless of the market cycle.
- The transformational actions are expected to have a very positive impact in the marketplace.

Overall, the report indicates a focus on optimizing financial performance, improving operational efficiencies, and capital management objectives while addressing challenges and opportunities in various business segments.
