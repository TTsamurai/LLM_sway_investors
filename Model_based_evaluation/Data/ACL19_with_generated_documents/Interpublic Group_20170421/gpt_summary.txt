The earnings call provided key financial metrics for the company, including organic revenue growth of 2.7% in the first quarter, with 2.9% in the US and 2.2% in international markets. Operating profit was $30 million, a $7 million increase compared to the previous year. Diluted EPS was $0.05, compared to $0.01 in the previous year. 

Revenue for the quarter was $1.75 billion, with organic growth of 2.7%. The US organic growth was 2.9%, while international markets had varying organic growth rates, with the UK at 4.4%, Continental Europe at 6.7%, Asia Pac with a decrease of 2.7%, and LatAm at 3.7%.

Total operating expenses increased by 30 basis points from the previous year, resulting in an operating margin expansion of 40 basis points. The total headcount increased by approximately 1%.

Cash used in operations in Q1 was $372 million, compared with $654 million a year ago. The company had $778 million in cash and short-term marketable securities at the end of the first quarter, compared with $680 million a year ago. Debt decreased to $1.92 billion at the most recent quarter-end.

Overall, the company is pleased with the solid revenue growth and profit performance in the quarter, which represents a good start in achieving their financial objectives for the full year.
