Key financial metrics from the recent earnings conference call transcripts include:
- Fourth quarter earnings were $415 million or $0.22 per diluted share, while full-year 2016 earnings were a loss of $497 million.
- Excluding special items and foreign exchange, Chevron earned $1.8 billion in 2016.
- Debt ratio at year-end was 24% and net debt stood at $39 billion, resulting in a net debt ratio of approximately 21%.
- Cash generated from operations was $3.9 billion during the fourth quarter and $12.8 billion on a year-to-date basis.
- Operating cash flow was affected by low oil and gas prices and weaker downstream margins in 2016.
- Capital expenditures were $4 billion for the fourth quarter and about $18 billion for the full year.
- Fourth quarter sales were approximately $870 million lower than the third quarter, and full year 2016 results were a loss of $497 million, approximately $5 billion lower than 2015 results.
- Upstream earnings decreased $798 million between periods, while downstream results decreased by $2.8 billion primarily due to lower margins.
- Interest expenses are expected to continue to grow in 2017 due to higher debt balances.
- Tax losses and carrybacks are expected to contribute cash in 2017.
- Annual per share dividends increased for the 29th consecutive year, totaling $8 billion in 2016.
- Cash and cash equivalents totaled $7 billion at year-end.
