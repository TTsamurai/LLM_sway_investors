Summary Report:

Earnings Discussion:
- Second quarter 2017 earnings were $0.85 per share compared to $0.74 per share in the same quarter of 2016.
- Weather increased electric margins in the second quarter of 2017 but had a negative impact on earnings quarter-over-quarter.
- Earnings per share for the six months ended June 30, 2017, were $2.04 per share versus $1.97 for the same period in 2016.
- Weather decreased earnings by $0.20 per share for the first six months of 2017.

Economic Trends:
- Companies announced plans to invest approximately $480 million with the expectation of creating over 7500 jobs in North and South Carolina territories.
- South Carolina's unemployment rate is 4%, the lowest since December of 2000.
- North Carolina and Georgia also continue to see declining unemployment rates of 4.2% and 4.8%, respectively.

Customer Growth and Electric Sales:
- SCE&G's Electric business added customers at a year-over-year rate of 1.6%.
- Regulated gas businesses in South and North Carolina added customers at a rate of 2.9% and 2.6%, respectively.
- Weather normalized kilowatt hour sales to retail customers for the 12 months ended June 30, 2017, were higher by eight tenths of a percent versus the prior period.

Regulatory Rate Base and Returns:
- Regulated rate base is approximately $11.5 billion.
- SCE&G base electric business is allowed a tentative quarter-percent return on equity.
- PSNC Energy's 12-month earned return was 10.78%.

2017 Earnings Guidance:
- Reiterating 2017 GAAP adjusted, weather-normalized earnings guidance range of $4.15 to $4.35 per share.
- O&M expense projected to be relatively flat due to labor and related savings.
- Long-term GAAP adjusted, weather-normalized annual growth guidance target remains unchanged at 4% to 6%.

Growth Strategy:
- Focus on growing PSNC Energy using the integrity management rider and periodic rate cases.
- Rates stabilization increases may be needed to maintain a reasonable earned return at the SCE&G business.
- Focus on O&M control, particularly at SCE&G, and benefit from lower projecting interest expense.

Wind Down Activities:
- Plans to focus strategy on the core electric gas business and on the redeployment of capital.
- Assessing tax implications of the abandoned asset and confident in the tax position.
