Some key financial metrics to highlight from the conference call include:

- Adjusted earnings per diluted share increased to $0.06 compared to a loss of $0.38 in the prior year's second quarter.
- Adjusted operating cash flow attributable to EQT increased by $118 million to $223 million.
- Production sales volume was 198 Bcfe, a 7% increase from the second quarter of 2016.
- The realized price, including cash settled derivatives, was $2.86 per Mcfe, reflecting a 36% increase compared to the prior year.
- Operating revenue for the production company totaled $631 million, a $554 million increase from the prior year.
- Operating expenses at EQT Production increased by 10% quarter-over-quarter.
- NGL sales volume and realized price were significantly higher.
- EQT Gathering operating income was $83.3 million, a $10.1 million increase from the prior year.
- EQT Transmission firm reservation fee revenue was $79.5 million, a $19.2 million increase from the prior year.
- The company reported a strong liquidity position with zero net short-term debt outstanding under EQT's $1.5 billion unsecured revolver and about $561 million of cash on the balance sheet.
- The company is forecasting $1.2 billion of operating cash flow for 2017 at EQT, which includes approximately $200 million of distributions to EQT from EQGP.

The conference call also mentioned the Rice Energy acquisition and potential future plans, including addressing some-of-the-parts issues in 2018 and the impact on capital deployment for drilling. These discussions provide additional context for the company's financial performance.
