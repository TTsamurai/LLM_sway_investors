Key financial metrics revealed in the earnings conference call transcript for Seagate include:

1. Addressable HDD market was in line with forecast
2. HDD enterprise revenues were up 3% year-over-year
3. Exabyte growth of 20% year-over-year
4. Nearline revenues were up 9% year-over-year
5. Hyperscale nearline revenues were up strong double digits
6. Revenues from high-capacity growth opportunities were up 25% year-over-year
7. Average capacity per drive across these markets was over 2 terabyte per drive, up 22% year-over-year
8. Operating expenses for the March quarter were $550 million on a GAAP basis and $443 million on a non-GAAP basis
9. Capital expenditures were $95 million for the March quarter
10. Cash flow from operations in the March quarter was $426 million and free cash flow was $331 million
11. The balance sheet remains healthy, with $3 billion in cash and cash equivalents
12. Quarterly dividend payment of $0.63 for the March quarter
13. Debt offering of $1.25 billion with a weighted average interest rate of less than 5%
14. Interest expense for the March quarter was $60 million and will be similar in the June quarter
15. Approximately 80% of Storage product revenue opportunities in calendar 2017 and beyond have growth potential

Based on the strong financial performance and growth potential in the Storage product revenue opportunities, an ‘overweight’ investment stance is recommended for Seagate. The company’s ability to generate strong cash flow and the healthy balance sheet position them for continued growth and profitability. Additionally, the cost alignment activities and competitiveness of the HDD product portfolio are expected to benefit product gross margins and overall profitability.
