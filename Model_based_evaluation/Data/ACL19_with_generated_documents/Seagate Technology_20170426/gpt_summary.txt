Key Financial Metrics:

1. Revenue: Seagate's total consolidated revenue saw a year-over-year increase, driven by growth in HDD enterprise revenues and strong double-digit growth in hyperscale nearline revenues. Revenues from high-capacity growth opportunities in consumer, surveillance, DDR, and NAS markets were up 25% year-over-year.

2. Operating Expenses: Operating expenses for the March quarter were $550 million on a GAAP basis and $443 million on a non-GAAP basis.

3. Capital Expenditures: Capital expenditures for the March quarter were $95 million for maintenance capital and manufacturing footprint redeployment, supporting the continued ramp of new HDD products in the portfolio.

4. Cash Flow: Cash flow from operations in the March quarter was $426 million, and free cash flow was $331 million. Excluding certain items, cash flow from operations would have been approximately $576 million.

5. Balance Sheet: Seagate ended the March quarter with $3 billion in cash and cash equivalents and 297 million ordinary shares outstanding.

6. Debt: Seagate completed a debt offering of $1.25 billion of investment-grade financing with a weighted average interest rate of less than 5%. The company has also called its 2021 7% senior notes with a payment of $158 million scheduled for May 1.

7. Dividend: The board approved a quarterly dividend payment of $0.63 for the March quarter, payable on July 5.

Overall, Seagate's March quarter results reflect strong execution of its business model objectives and the ability to generate strong cash flow, while also focusing on cost alignment activities to improve product gross margins and overall profitability.
