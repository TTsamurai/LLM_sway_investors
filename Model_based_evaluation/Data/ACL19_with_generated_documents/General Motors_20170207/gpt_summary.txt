Based on the recent earnings conference call transcripts, key financial metrics included record EBIT-adjusted of $12.5 billion, up $1.7 billion year-over-year, record net revenue of $166.4 billion, up $14 billion year-over-year, and EBIT-adjusted margin of 7.5%, up 40 basis points year-over-year. North America achieved record revenue of $119 billion for the year, with an EBIT-adjusted margin of 10.1%. GM Financial posted record revenue of $9.6 billion and record earnings before tax adjusted of $900 million, up $100 million year-over-year. Automotive free cash flow grew to $6.9 billion for the year, up $4.7 billion year-over-year. The company returned $4.8 billion to shareholders throughout the year, including $2.3 billion in common stock dividends and $2.5 billion in share repurchases. The company expects full-year 2017 EPS diluted adjusted of $6 to $6.50 and anticipates generating about $15 billion in automotive operating cash flow and $6 billion in automotive adjusted free cash flow. The company is supportive of efforts by President Trump and Congress to implement tax reform that improves the competitiveness of American companies. However, there is significant uncertainty about the potential implications of border adjustment tax and other tax reform proposals. Despite a $300 million impact from Brexit, there was an improvement in losses in Europe. The company is focused on mitigating the impact of Brexit and expects relatively flat performance in Europe in 2017. The company expects to see improved equity income in China and continued improvement in South America throughout 2017. Overall, the company is confident in meeting its commitments in 2017 as it has done over the past three years.
